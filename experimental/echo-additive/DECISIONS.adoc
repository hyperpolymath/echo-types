== D-2026-06-14-R1: Comparative protocol — primary and secondary lines

*Date:* 2026-06-14 +
*Author authorization:* explicit, in-session +
*Supersedes:* none (first decision for this tree)

=== Decision

R1 is resolved as a *comparative protocol*, not as a choice of one reading over the
other. Both the monadic and comonadic readings of the grade composition map are to
be constructed in parallel in the experimental tree. Neither is declared the winner
before construction.

=== Rationale

The variance analysis at R1 established:

* The combining direction of Echo-comp-iso has monadic variance (μ direction;
  `D_r(D_s A) → D_{r+s} A`). Building this gives the primary line.
* The splitting direction has comonadic variance (δ direction;
  `D_{r+s} A → D_r(D_s A)`). Building this gives the secondary line.

The question of whether *recoverability* — given a lossy output, what inputs were
possible? — lives naturally in the monadic direction, the comonadic direction, or
genuinely requires both, cannot be settled by structural analysis alone. It requires
attempting the laws in `--safe --without-K` Agda and seeing what can and cannot be
discharged. Choosing before construction would discard potentially live evidence.

=== Three enforcement specifics

[cols="1,4", options="header"]
|===
| Enforcement specific | Binding constraint

| *WHERE*
| Scope is `proofs/agda/experimental/echo-additive/` exclusively. Shipped modules
(`EchoGraded.agda`, `EchoGradedComonad.agda`, `EchoTruncation.agda`) are read-only.
Nothing in shipped code imports from experimental/. R-2026-05-18 retraction stands.

| *WHAT HOLDS YOU (the gate)*
| Nothing from experimental/ goes to main — no merge, no claim, no retraction
reversal — until one of the two terminating conditions is met AND the author
explicitly authorizes the merge. "Both lines compile" alone does not satisfy the gate.
Author authorization alone does not satisfy the gate. Both required together.

| *LINE-SEPARATION FIREWALL*
| Primary (`GradedMonad.agda`) and secondary (`GradedComonad.agda`) are separate files.
Neither imports the other during the law-discharge phase. No postulates anywhere in
experimental/ (a postulate in one line corrupts the comparison). Synthesis happens in
a dedicated file created only after both lines have been independently evaluated.
|===

=== Terminating condition

[cols="1,4", options="header"]
|===
| Case | Consequence

| *(i)* One reading discharges its laws; the other provably cannot (park point reached
  with named obstruction and no postulate escape)
| The discharging reading is the correct structural home. The other is parked with the
  named obstruction. Author decides whether to proceed to R4+ on the surviving line.

| *(ii)* Both readings discharge their laws
| Proceed to adjunction proof: construct F_r, U_r, and the witness `F_r ⊣ U_r`
  in `--safe --without-K` Agda. The adjunction is *not assumed* from the law
  discharge — it must be proven as a separate construction. Only after the witness
  compiles does this satisfy (ii). If the adjunction proof itself fails (postulate
  needed), document the obstruction explicitly and park. Report to author; do not
  auto-merge.
|===
