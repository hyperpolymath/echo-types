= Adjacency: quotient types / setoid quotients
:toc: macro

[.lead]
*Verdict: distinct.* Both the truncation and 2-cell arguments are
active and formalised against this neighbour. This is the gate-1
case argued in most depth.

toc::[]

== Nearest existing construction

For surjective `f : A ‚Üí B`, the kernel `ker f` is an equivalence
relation and `B ‚ČÖ A / ker f`. The equivalence class of `x` under
`ker f` is the same data as `Echo f (f x)`. The construction is
the same; the *stance* differs:

* Quotient theory studies the *codomain that results from
  collapse*. The quotient `A / ker f` is a set whose elements are
  classes; individual witnesses are projected away.
* Echo theory studies the *witnesses to collapse*. `Echo f y` is
  a type whose elements are concrete `(x, p)` pairs; the
  individual `x` is preserved.

This is the duality that makes both arguments fire.

== Distinctness claim

[quote]
Echo types carry witness data that quotient theory discards by
construction (truncation argument), and the natural 2-cells in
sophisticated quotient encodings are themselves ő£-over-preimages-
shaped (2-cell argument).

== Truncation argument

Quotients identify witnesses by definition: the quotient class of
`x` is `[x]`, with no remembered witness identity. Echo retains
witness identity as the first component of the ő£.

Formal exhibits (gate-3 examples):

* `EpistemicUpdate.refine-injective` uses individual world
  identity, which the quotient does not provide.
* `EpistemicUpdate.distinct-w‚āĀ-w‚āÉ` exhibits two distinct worlds
  in the same quotient class, formally distinguishable in Echo.
* `EpistemicUpdate.echo-not-prop` certifies the knowledge type is
  not a mere proposition, so propositional-class accounts cannot
  reproduce it.

The general truncation theorem (Q2.1): for non-injective `f` with
multiple preimages of `y`, `is-prop (Echo f y) ‚Üí ‚ä•`. Quotients
*are* the proposition that loses what this theorem certifies.

== 2-cell argument

Naive quotient stacking fails on the simplest cross-axis
non-commutation:

* `EchoVsQuotient.Miniature.kernel-disagreement` ‚ÄĒ concrete
  witness in the two-bit setting: `ker(g‚āĀ‚ąėrL) ‚Č† ker(rL‚ąėg‚āĀ)` at
  the witness pair `((true, true), (false, true))`.

Sophisticated quotient encodings have been attempted and sealed:

* *2-categorical encoding* (most plausible sophisticated route):
  `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.equalizer‚Üíecho-pair` shows the
  natural 2-cell (StrictComparison = equalizer of two parallel
  maps) is structurally ő£-over-preimages ‚ÄĒ i.e., already
  echo-shaped. Any 2-cell witness decomposes mechanically into
  two Echo witnesses sharing a preimage.
* *Joins of kernels* (S-1):
  `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.Joins.join-universal` shows the
  join of the two non-commuting kernels is the universal
  relation on the carrier, so `A / Join` is a singleton ‚ÄĒ joins
  collapse and lose all integration content.
* *Indexed families with coherence laws* (S-2):
  `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.IndexedFamilies.s-2-reduction`
  shows coherence-law data unfolds definitionally to
  `StrictComparison`, so the same `equalizer‚Üíecho-pair`
  conversion applies. The argument generalises mechanically to
  larger indices.

Two further sub-cases (S-3 axiomatic 2-cells, S-4 HoTT/HiTs)
have informal arguments only ‚ÄĒ see Q1.6 in `next-questions.adoc`.

== What echo adds

Witness identity (the first projection of the ő£) and the natural
2-cell as data rather than a free-floating coherence axiom.

== What echo does not add

The *equivalence relation* itself is the same. If the only
operation needed is "are these two things in the same class?",
quotients suffice and echo is overkill. Gate-3's framing makes
this explicit: echo wins for *dynamic / programmable* knowledge
update; the two are tied for *static / classical* knowledge
analysis.

== Retraction conditions

Either of the following retracts gate-1's distinctness claim
against quotient types:

. A quotient encoding of the characteristic-family commutation
  theorem (`RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute` or its successor) that
  uses no echo machinery.
. A demonstration that the 2-cell argument's ő£-shape conclusion
  is itself derivable from generic stdlib lemmas about quotients
  (i.e., the structural argument is a fiber-lemma in disguise).

Neither has been exhibited.
