= Adjacency: Refinement Types
:toc: macro

toc::[]

== 1. Nearest existing construction

Refinement types carve out a subset of an ambient type by a predicate:

[source]
----
{ x : A | P x }
----

Inhabitants are values of `A` together with (in practice, erased) proof
that `P x` holds. The paradigmatic developments are LiquidHaskell,
F*, Stainless, and Dafny. In a dependently typed setting the refinement
subset type is definitionally `Σ (x : A) , P x` with `P x : Prop`.

== 2. What do echo types add?

The echo construction is _also_ a sigma, but the right component is an
equation against a _named_ output:

[source, agda]
----
Echo f y := Σ (x : A) , (f x ≡ y)
----

Three features separate it from the refinement-type reading in the
current development:

. *Extensional indexing by a post-image rather than by an arbitrary
  predicate.* The constraint is `f x ≡ y`, tied to the function whose
  loss is being studied. This yields a family `Echo f : B → Set`, not a
  single subset. The family structure is what makes `map-over` and
  `map-square` in `proofs/agda/Echo.agda` type-check as transport along
  morphisms between families, rather than as weakening of a single
  predicate.
. *Proof-relevant distinctions over the same visible output.*
  `echo-true≢echo-false` in `proofs/agda/EchoCharacteristic.agda` shows
  two inhabitants of `Echo collapse tt` that are _not_ equal. In a
  refinement-type reading the proposition `P` would typically be
  proof-irrelevant, and `{ x : ⊤ | ⊤ }` would be contractible. The
  echo layer deliberately retains the source witness.
. *Strict weakening to residue.* `strict-weakening-collapse` in
  `proofs/agda/EchoResidue.agda` exhibits a lowering `Echo → EchoR`
  with no section. Refinement-type presentations usually collapse at
  the propositional level and do not admit an analogous hierarchy of
  strictly weaker certificates.
. *Compositional structure as theorems, not as user-supplied glue.*
  `Echo-comp-iso-{to, from, from-to, to-from}` in `proofs/agda/Echo.agda`
  proves `Echo (g ∘ f) y ≃ Σ b. Echo f b × (g b ≡ y)` directly. With a
  bijection on the outer leg, `cancel-iso-{to, from}` exhibits the
  cancellation `Echo (g ∘ f) y → Echo f (s y)` and back; the projection-
  pentagon lemmas `Echo-comp-iso-pent-{B, echo}` show two natural
  three-fold factorings agree. None of this is standard refinement-type
  machinery: refinement subsets have no ambient theorem stating that
  `{ x | (g ∘ f) x = y }` is canonically isomorphic to a Σ over an
  intermediate refinement, because the predicate carrier does not
  isolate the intermediate point as proof-relevant data.

== 3. What do echo types _not_ add?

* No new subset-type discipline. Refinement types already subsume the
  pattern "value of `A` plus certificate".
* No new predicate logic. The echo equation `f x ≡ y` is a single
  intensional equality; refinement types support arbitrary decidable
  predicates, and are strictly more expressive at that level.
* No automation. Tools like LiquidHaskell emphasise SMT-backed
  inference; this development is manual Agda proofs.

== 4. Honest risk to the distinction

The sharpest adversarial reading is: "an echo type is just the
refinement `{ x : A | f x = y }`, and your proof-relevance claim only
survives because you chose an intensional equality as the predicate."
Under that reading, the "addition" of proof-relevant distinctions is
a property of how equality behaves in `--without-K` Agda, not of
echo types as such.

The residue-based strict weakening in `EchoResidue` is the part most
resistant to this reading: a refinement type has no natural
counterpart to "lower to a certificate, and prove no section exists".
The composition-iso family is the second load-bearing piece: it
states a property of the family `Echo f` that has no obvious
refinement-subset counterpart unless one re-imports dependent-pair
machinery, at which point the refinement reading is no longer
faithful.

== 5. Verdict for Gate 1

Survives, with caveat: the distinction from refinement types rests
materially on (a) the family indexing by `f`, (b) the residue
hierarchy, and (c) the composition / cancellation / pentagon iso
family — rather than on the subset-type form itself.
