= Adjacency: HoTT homotopy fibers
:toc: macro

[.lead]
*Verdict: not distinct from HoTT homotopy fibers — echo IS the
homotopy fiber.* The win is over the *propositional truncation
modality* applied to fibers, not over fibers themselves. This is
the most-coincident neighbour in the directory.

This note exists to state the coincidence plainly rather than
gloss over it. Overstating distinctness here would be the
clearest way to lose credibility on gate #1.

toc::[]

== The honest claim, up front

`Echo f y := Σ A (λ x → f x ≡ y)` *is* the homotopy fiber of `f`
at `y`, by definition. There is no syntactic or semantic
difference. Anyone fluent in HoTT will recognise the construction
on first sight.

Distinctness against HoTT-fiber-as-construction is *not* a
defensible claim. Distinctness against HoTT-fiber-as-construction-
post-truncation *is*, but the truncation is an applied modality,
not part of the fiber's primitive definition.

== Distinctness claim (narrow)

[quote]
Echo coincides with the HoTT homotopy fiber definitionally. The
distinctness claim against HoTT is not against the construction
itself, but against accounts that *truncate* the fiber to a
proposition before further analysis. `echo-not-prop` certifies
the truncation loses witness data.

This is genuinely the right framing. The previous consolidated
doc said "Cubical fibers are the same construction with stricter
equality. Different setting, same shape." This was correct as a
description but understated how completely the constructions
coincide.

== Truncation argument

The HoTT framework provides a propositional-truncation modality
`‖ - ‖`. Some HoTT-flavoured uses of fibers apply this modality
("there merely exists a witness" rather than "here is a witness
type"), at which point the witness-type data is lost.

`echo-not-prop` certifies that echo types (= homotopy fibers)
are *not invariant* under this truncation when the underlying
function is non-injective. So:

* `Echo f y` (= `fib f y`) — *not* a mere proposition for
  non-injective `f` with multiple preimages of `y`.
* `‖ Echo f y ‖` (= truncation of the fiber) — a mere
  proposition by construction.

Echo wins against the truncated form by definition (it's
literally the un-truncated form). This is true for both
HoTT-as-foundations and HoTT-as-applied-tool.

The win is therefore not "echo vs. HoTT" but "echo (= bare
fiber) vs. propositionally-truncated fiber". HoTT *contains*
both; the win is over a specific applied use of HoTT, not over
HoTT itself.

== Composition-iso evidence

Beyond the `echo-not-prop` witness-loss certificate, the iso
family in `proofs/agda/Echo.agda` provides structural evidence
for the proof-relevant-vs-truncated distinction under
composition:

* `Echo-comp-iso-{to, from, from-to, to-from}` exhibits
  `Echo (g ∘ f) y ≃ Σ b. Echo f b × (g b ≡ y)` with both
  round-trips on the nose.
* `cancel-iso-{to, from}` reduce composition through a section
  to composition through the underlying fiber, when the outer
  step admits a pointwise inverse.
* `Echo-comp-iso-pent-{B, echo}` show two natural three-fold
  factorings agree by `refl` on the A-origin and the inner
  echo.

These are not new HoTT results — they are corollaries of
Σ-associativity and J — but they ship as named theorems in plain
Agda with a deliberately minimal `--without-K` proof. Under
propositional truncation the family collapses (Σ becomes ∃, and
the round-trip equations become automatic since the targets are
propositions); in the un-truncated form, the family preserves
witness identity under composition. Together with
`echo-not-prop`, this is the second structural distinction
between proof-relevant fibres and truncated fibres: not just
that the witness data is present, but that it composes without
intermediate higher-equalities.

The fact is structural about Echo-as-fiber and is independent
of EI-2's negative termination — it distinguishes proof-relevant
fibres from truncated fibres regardless of what the integration
recipe does or doesn't do.

== 2-cell argument

In HoTT (or cubical Agda), 2-cells between parallel fibers are
*paths between path-types*, structurally an iterated equalizer.
The `Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair` argument from
`quotients.adoc` applies trivially: HoTT 2-cells *are* equalizers
definitionally.

So the 2-cell argument doesn't separately fire against HoTT — it
fires *through* HoTT, using HoTT's own primitives. Echo's
distinctness on the 2-cell axis is against neighbours that *don't*
have HoTT-style equalizers natively (quotients before HIT, AI
without lattice meets, etc.); HoTT itself is the framework in
which the argument is most natural.

== What echo adds (over bare HoTT fibers)

Nothing structural. Echo is an *organising vocabulary* for
fiber-shaped reasoning — it gives names to things HoTT users
already work with under different names ("homotopy fiber",
"path-space over a point", etc.). The benefit is in the *systematic
treatment of the recipe* (custom decoration order + echo-indexed
family + transport action) on multiple axes; HoTT users can do
each of these but typically don't have a unified vocabulary for
them.

This is a genuine contribution at the *exposition* level, not at
the *foundations* level. Worth being explicit about.

== What echo adds (over propositionally-truncated fibers)

* Witness identity (the Σ's first projection, retained).
* The ability to define functions on the witness type.
* Non-truncatability for non-injective functions
  (`echo-not-prop`).

This is the same `echo-not-prop`-shaped win as against
propositional refinement and relational IFC. The neighbour-
specific fact is that HoTT contains the truncation as a
*modality* rather than as a baked-in choice — so a HoTT user can
*choose* not to truncate, recovering echo's data automatically.

== Recipe-level distinctness

The recipe (decoration order + echo-indexed family + transport
action) is fully expressible in HoTT and cubical type theory.
Cubical Agda in particular makes the transport part definitionally
nice. There is no recipe-level distinctness against HoTT.

What HoTT users *typically don't do* is apply the recipe
systematically across the five (or six) axes echo identifies.
The recipe is not natively named or studied as a unit in the HoTT
literature; this is what echo organises. Again, an exposition
contribution, not a foundational one.

== Cubical Agda specifically

The S-4 sub-case in `next-questions.adoc` Q1.6 — higher inductive
types in cubical Agda — is the case where HoTT's added strength
*could* cleanly express echo-shaped 2-cells natively, at the cost
of importing the full HoTT framework. The S-4 closure (informal)
is: importing HoTT exceeds "pure quotient theory" by a much wider
margin than echo types, so this isn't a win for the neighbour.

The HoTT-side framing is just the converse: HoTT contains echo
machinery (= fibers) trivially, plus a great deal more. For a HoTT
user, echo is a tiny corner of an existing framework; for an
echo user, HoTT is overkill.

== Retraction conditions

This is the one neighbour where retraction is essentially
guaranteed: echo *is* the homotopy fiber. The narrow distinctness
claim — against the propositionally-truncated form — is the only
defensible position.

Specific retraction triggers:

. *Any* claim that echo is "different from" the homotopy fiber
  rather than "different from the truncated homotopy fiber"
  should be retracted on sight. The previous consolidated doc
  did not contain such a claim, but this note exists partly to
  prevent one creeping in.
. A demonstration that `echo-not-prop` follows trivially from a
  HoTT lemma about fibers of non-injective functions. This would
  not retract distinctness against truncated fibers (the
  truncation still loses data) but would mean the echo-side
  formalisation is just re-deriving a known HoTT lemma. Worth
  acknowledging if it turns out to be the case.

== Open obligation

There is no dedicated `proofs/agda/adjacency/HoTT.agda`. There
likely shouldn't be — formalising "echo IS the homotopy fiber"
is just `refl` after unfolding definitions. The truncation
argument is already formalised via gate-3's `echo-not-prop`
family.

The open work here is *exposition*, not formalisation: making
sure the README, the gate-1 distinctness doc, and any future
papers are honest about the coincidence rather than overclaiming.

== Status

* Coincidence with bare HoTT fibers: *acknowledged as coincidence*,
  not a distinctness claim.
* Truncation argument (against truncated fibers): *formalised*
  via gate-3's `echo-not-prop` family.
* Recipe-level distinctness: *N/A* (HoTT can express the recipe
  natively; the exposition contribution is acknowledged as
  exposition).
* No dedicated Agda formalisation needed.
