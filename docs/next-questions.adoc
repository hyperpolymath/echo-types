= Open questions
:toc: macro
:source-highlighter: rouge

This document collects the open questions surfaced across the three
gates' work. Each question carries a *home gate* (whose territory it
belongs to), an *attack path* (concrete steps to make progress), and
where applicable an *explicit retraction condition* (what would mean
the underlying claim should be retracted).

Questions are organised by gate. A *cross-cutting* section collects
questions that span multiple gates or concern integration polish.
A *closed* section at the end records questions that have been
discharged, with pointers to the formalisations that closed them.

Per the gates protocol in `roadmap-gates.adoc`, retraction is the
mechanism by which the identity claim is made falsifiable. A
question whose retraction condition is met *retracts* the
corresponding claim; it does not weaken into a hedge.

== Gate 1: distinct phenomenon

=== Q1.1 — Galois reduction

Does there exist a Galois-theoretic construction that expresses the
characteristic-family commutation theorem (currently
`RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`) without importing graded or
choreographic primitives?

Status::
Partially answered. `EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated` formalises that
the natural Galois 2-cell (the meet in the abstraction lattice) is
Σ-over-preimages with a paired equality predicate
(`meet→echo-intersection`), structurally equivalent to the
quotient case via the diagonal correspondence
(`meet-on-diagonal→equalizer`). Higher-order Galois constructions
(functor-category insertions, Galois-of-Galois) reduce to
`IndexedFamilies` (S-2) by a parallel argument; not separately
formalised.

Attack path::
Either (a) instantiate `T1-obligation` in `EchoVsGalois.agda` with
`choreo-grade-commute`'s actual signature and complete a Galois-
only proof — gate #1 falsified — or (b) extract the precise stuck
goal as a distinctness witness.

Retraction::
If (a) succeeds without importing primitives outside abstract
interpretation, gate #1 is falsified by T1.

=== Q1.2 — Quotient reduction

Does quotient stacking (or any sophisticated quotient encoding)
express the characteristic-family commutation theorem without
importing echo machinery?

Status::
Substantially answered. The C1/C2/C3 decomposition has C1 closed
(`Miniature.kernel-disagreement`), the 2-categorical case of C3
closed (`Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair`), S-1 closed
(`Sophisticated.Joins.join-universal`), and S-2 closed
(`Sophisticated.IndexedFamilies.s-2-reduction`). S-3 (axiomatic
2-cells) and S-4 (HoTT/HiTs) closed only informally; both face
structural barriers to safe-Agda formalisation. See Q1.6 for the
sub-case state.

Attack path::
Same as Q1.1: instantiate `T2-obligation` with
`choreo-grade-commute`'s signature and either complete a
quotient-only proof or extract the stuck goal.

Retraction::
If a quotient encoding without echo machinery succeeds on the
characteristic-family commutation theorem, gate #1 is falsified
by T2.

Status::

unstubbed. Awaiting stable signatures from `EchoEpistemic` and
`EchoLinear` before attempting.

Attack path::

correspondingly.

Refined target (after gate-3's RoleGraded landing)::

== Gate 2: characteristic theorems

=== Q2.1 — Generalisation of `echo-not-prop`

Gate-3's three canonical examples each carry a constructive proof
that the relevant `Echo` type is not a mere proposition
(`TropicalArgmin.echo-not-prop`, `EpistemicUpdate.echo-not-prop`,
`LinearErasure.echo-not-prop`). Each is the n=2 special case of:

[quote]
For `f : A → B` and `y : B`, if there exist `x₁ ≠ x₂ : A` with
`f x₁ ≡ y` and `f x₂ ≡ y`, then `is-prop (Echo f y) → ⊥`.

This is naturally echo-shaped because it is about the *type*
`Echo f y`, not about a single inhabitant. Whether it earns
characteristic status depends on the gate-2 bar.

Status::
Open. Forwarded by gate-3's `gate-3-handoff.adoc` for gate-2
evaluation.

Attack path::
. State and prove the general theorem in
  `proofs/agda/characteristic/NonTruncatable.agda`.
. Audit it against the gate-2 falsifier ("naturally echo-shaped,
  not reducible to generic Σ/fiber lemmas").
. If it survives, add to `roadmap-gates.adoc` Gate 2 nominee table
  as a candidate N6 (or N5 if `choreo-grade-commute` is not
  separately adopted).

Internal falsifier::
If the general theorem follows by routine unfolding from a stdlib
lemma about Σ-types having distinct elements, it is not echo-
specific; it is just "a Σ with two distinct inhabitants is not
propositional".

with `cong proj₁` is injective on first projections.

=== Q2.5 — `retained-shape` as characteristic

Gate-3's handoff flagged `LinearErasure.retained-shape` as a
candidate characteristic theorem family member:

[source,agda]
----
retained-shape :
  ∀ {P Q} (e : Echo erase (fn P Q)) → is-fn-type (proj₁ e)
----

The proof is *pattern matching on the source type*, not extraction
of `proj₂`. It is an inversion lemma: from "erasure of T equals
this function-shape", recover that "T itself is a function-shape".

Status::
Open. Forwarded by gate-3's handoff for gate-2 evaluation.

Internal falsifier::
If the inversion follows from a generic stdlib lemma about
Σ-types of the form `Σ A (λ a → f a ≡ y)` admitting case-analysis
on `a`, it is not echo-specific.

Attack path::
. Attempt the falsifier (try to derive `retained-shape` from a
  generic stdlib Σ-case-analysis lemma).
. If the falsifier fails, audit the lemma against the gate-2 bar
  in `docs/characteristic.adoc`.

== Closed questions

=== Q1.5 — Concrete counter-example for naive quotient stacking

CLOSED. Smallest counter-example constructed in
`proofs/agda/EchoVsQuotient.agda` § `Miniature`:
`kernel-disagreement` proves that ker(g₁∘rL) ≠ ker(rL∘g₁) in the
two-bit setting `A = Bool × Bool` with AND-collapse and
first-bit projection. The witness pair is
((true, true), (false, true)).

The full T2 obligation decomposes into C1, C2, C3; C1 is what
this answer closes. C2 is trivially true. C3 is decomposed in
Q1.6 above; the load-bearing sub-cases are themselves closed.

=== Q1.7 — Galois 2-cell escape

CLOSED. The Galois reduction (T1) does not admit a 2-cell
construction escaping the Σ-over-preimages shape. Formalised in
`proofs/agda/EchoVsGalois.agda` § `Sophisticated`:

* `meet→echo-intersection` shows the meet's data unpacks
  directly into two Echo witnesses sharing a preimage.
* `meet-on-diagonal→equalizer` establishes that the AI meet
  specialises to the quotient equalizer when output values
  coincide; off the diagonal, the meet is strictly more general
  but structurally identical.

The remaining sub-cases (lattice-degeneracy analog of S-1;
higher-order Galois constructions analog of S-2) reduce by
structural argument to the quotient-side closures already
formalised in `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.Joins` and
`Sophisticated.IndexedFamilies`.

=== EI-1 — Genuine cross-decoration integration theorem

CLOSED (protocol). Open work item flagged in `gate-2-handoff.adoc`
Observation E (now retitled "Observation E — *closed*"): a
single Echo-indexed family carrying both a role/choreographic
decoration and a graded decoration, with conjuncts sharing data.
Constructed in `proofs/agda/characteristic/RoleGraded.agda`:

* `applyRole`, `applyGrade` — two independent actions.
* `choreo-grade-commute` — the integration theorem; both

Honest qualification (not a re-opening)::

2D family is asymmetric — the role parameter is only live at
keep grade. EI-1 is therefore *protocol-closed but substantively
narrow*. The follow-up question — is the recipe robust across

=== EI-2 — Robustness of the integration recipe under richer 2D combinations

CLOSED (negatively, via PATH B). The integration recipe with the
existing five named axes does *not* carry substantive simultaneous
cross-axis content. Eight phases of investigation produced seven
data points, all consistent with this finding:

[cols="2,1,1,3", options="header"]
|===
| Construction | Cells | Non-trivial | Reading

| `RoleGraded` (Role × Grade)
| 18
| 1
| Load-bearing cell `(c⊑s, keep≤keep)`; non-trivial content is
Choreo's, with Grade as identity.

| `RoleMode` (Role × Mode)
| 9
| 1
| Same shape; cell `(c⊑s, linear≤linear)`.

| `ModeGraded` (Mode × Grade)
| 18
| 0
| Both axes loss-only; no non-trivial cell exists.

| `RoleModeGrade` 3D pairwise
| 27, 36, 36
| 1, 1, 0
| 3D inherits from the constituent 2D pairs; no qualitative change.

| `RoleModeGrade` triple
| 54
| 1
| Same load-bearing cell.

| `RoleRole` independent-product
| 9
| 0
| Both axes non-loss-only; commutation trivial by categorical
product structure (no substantive cross-axis content).

| `RoleRole` shared-state
| —
| —
| Per-axis transport not uniformly definable; recipe doesn't apply.
|===

Across all data points, every "non-trivial" cell carries
*one-axis-at-a-time* content. The non-loss-only criterion (formal
certificate for Choreo in `ChoreoInjective.agda`) is necessary but
not sufficient for substantive simultaneous interaction.

Verdict::
The integration recipe does NOT carry the gate-1 distinctness
load. Distinctness rests on:
+
* The truncation argument: `echo-not-prop` family in
  `proofs/agda/examples/`, certifying that for non-injective `f`
  with multiple preimages of `y`, `Echo f y` is not a mere
  proposition.
* The 2-cell argument: `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair`
  and `EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated.meet→echo-intersection`,
  certifying that natural 2-cells in neighbour frameworks are
  Σ-over-preimages-shaped.
+
These two arguments stand independently and are unaffected by
EI-2's negative resolution.

Why PATH B (partial formalisation accepted as sufficient)::
The full 2D iff theorem is not formalisable in safe Agda without
postulates (decidable equality on decoration types, F-collapses
axioms, extensionality on cell actions). `RecipeTheorem.agda`
proves the per-axis halves; `RecipeNonTriviality.agda` proves
the concrete construction-by-construction halves. Together with
the seven data points and the structural argument from
`RoleRole.agda`, the empirical and structural evidence is
overwhelming. The formal generic theorem is documented as future
work; it is not required to settle the gate-1 distinctness
question, since the negative finding *removes* the integration
recipe from the distinctness load.

The positive-termination route (extending the recipe to allow
coupled state across axes or multiple live positions per axis)
is parked for v0.2 and beyond. A new question will track this
if/when it becomes active. EI-2 itself is closed; the extension
is genuinely separate work.


== Conventions

* Each open question carries a *home gate*. Cross-cutting
  questions are in their own section.
* Each open question carries an *attack path* and (where
  applicable) an *explicit retraction condition*.
* Closed questions are moved to the §<<closed,Closed questions>>
  section with a pointer to the formalisation that closed them.
* Numbering is gate-prefixed: Q1.x for gate-1, Q2.x for gate-2,
  Q3.x for gate-3, QC.x for cross-cutting. Numbers are stable
  across edits (a closed question keeps its number; a withdrawn
  question is marked withdrawn rather than removed).
