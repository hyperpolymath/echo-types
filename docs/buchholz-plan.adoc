= Buchholz-style Collapsing Layer: Plan and First Proof
:toc: macro
:toclevels: 2
:sectnums:
:sectnumlevels: 2

[.lead]
This document scopes the ordinal-notation / collapsing-function workstream
(Workstream E in `roadmap.adoc`). It is a spec, not a theorem file.
Its purpose is to pin down *what we are building*, in what order, and
what counts as the first success before any ψ-value is even mentioned.

toc::[]

== Mission

Formalize, in `--safe --without-K` Agda, a staged Buchholz-style
collapsing notation system sufficient to eventually state a target
such as `ψ₀(Ω_ω)` (Bachmann–Howard) and, beyond that, a compact
reading of `ψ(Ω_Ω)`.

Only when that syntactic engine exists do we add `EchoOrdinal.agda`
to bridge collapsing steps with the existing echo/residue machinery.

== Target selection

`ψ(Ω_Ω)` is an ambiguous phrase without further qualification.
We fix the following staged convention. Each stage is a gate: the
next stage is not attempted until the current stage compiles and
its headline lemma is established.

[cols="1,3,3", options="header"]
|===
| Stage | Target language | Intended witness term

| S0 | single-cardinal pedagogical ψ, ℕ-indexed closure stages | `ψ 0`, `ψ (ψ 0)`
| S1 | finite-index Buchholz fragment ψ_n over Ω_n                  | `ψ₀(Ω₁)`
| S2 | Buchholz ψ_ν for ν ≤ ω                                       | `ψ₀(Ω_ω)` (Bachmann–Howard)
| S3 | extended fragment sufficient to denote the intended `ψ(Ω_Ω)` | see S3 note
|===

S3 note: "ψ(Ω_Ω)" is formalised in this repo as `ψ₀(Ω_{Ω_ω})`,
i.e. Ω at the position of the Ω_ω marker in the Ω-scale. Other
readings are possible (diagonalization fixpoint, Rathjen-style
extensions); those are *not* what this plan targets.

== Layering

The workstream separates into three layers that must not be conflated.

. Notation infrastructure: term syntax, normal forms, comparison.
. Buchholz collapsing machinery: closure sets, ψ as least-gap.
. Echo bridge: collapsing step read as non-injective map, echoes
  retaining pre-collapse provenance.

Echo-types’ existing identity claim (structured loss under
non-injective computation) only plugs in at layer 3. Layers 1 and 2
must stand as ordinary proof-theoretic development first.

== WF track status (current)

The order / well-foundedness track now has an explicit staged split:

* WF-0 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.Order`): partial strict order with
  irreflexivity and transitivity over the currently admitted
  constructors.
* WF-1 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.WellFounded`): `WellFounded _<ᵇ_` for the
  current core, with irreflexivity recovered as a corollary
  (`<ᵇ-irreflexive`) instead of a direct shared-binder elimination
  proof.
* WF-1.5 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenInterface`): measure-route interface
  pinning constructor-by-constructor decrease obligations and explicit
  deferred same-binder obligations (`dec-ψα`, `dec-+2`) for the Veblen
  follow-up.
* WF-1.6 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenIdentityModel`): first concrete
  instantiation of the interface (identity-measure bootstrap), keeping
  `dec-ψα` and `dec-+2` as explicit assumptions.
* WF-1.65 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenMeasureTarget`): first concrete
  measure target fixed as a lexicographic order on `OmegaIndex × BT`,
  with well-foundedness reduced to `×-wellFounded <Ω-wf wf-<ᵇ`.
* WF-1.68 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenProjectionMeasure`): projection
  measure into that target made explicit; the two deferred
  shared-binder obligations now land as concrete target lemmas
  `proj-dec-+2` and `proj-dec-ψα`.
* WF-1.69 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenComparisonTarget`,
  `Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenComparisonModel`): comparison-lex target on
  `BT × Payload` with recursive `ψ`-compatibility on the first
  coordinate and tagged payload descent on the second; now packages
  an assumption-free interface model in which `dec-ψα`, `dec-+2`,
  and the lifted same-index `ψ`-to-`+` comparison are all discharged
  internally.
* WF-1.7 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.VeblenObligations`): discharge
  same-binder obligation shapes in projection-model form; landed lemmas:
  `dec-+2-plus-right`, `dec-ψα-psi-arg`.
* WF-1.8 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.ExtendedOrder`): comparison-induced
  wrapper relation `_ <ᵇ⁺ _` on `BT`, containing the current core and
  exposing same-binder principles as derived lemmas; transitivity and
  well-foundedness now come from the closed comparison target rather
  than direct constructor recursion.
* WF-1.85 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.SurfaceOrder`): first direct inductive
  surface `_ <ᵇˢ _` adding the two historical same-binder shapes as
  constructors over current-core premises; embeds into `_ <ᵇ⁺ _` and
  therefore inherits well-foundedness immediately.
* WF-1.86 (`SurfaceLiftInterface` / recursive `_ <ᵇʳ _` skeleton in
  `Ordinal.Buchholz.SurfaceOrder`): this candidate was tested and
  blocked. A concrete same-left `bplus` counterexample refutes the
  required lifted `ψ`-stability against the current wrapper, so the
  recursive surface route is not the right intermediate theorem.
  The direct surface bridge `_ <ᵇˢ _` remains the proved bridge
  candidate.
* WF-1.87 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.LiftedExtendedOrder`): the next honest
  step is wrapper iteration rather than wrapper self-lift. A new
  well-founded wrapper `_ <ᵇ⁺¹ _` lifts same-binder moves out of
  `_ <ᵇ⁺ _` and proves the first nested example
  `ψν (x + y) < ψν (x + z)` from `y <ᵇ z`.
* WF-1.88 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.IteratedExtendedOrder`): the one-step
  repair now scales uniformly to finite depth. `LiftedOrder n`
  iterates the wrapper construction over `ℕ`, `LiftedOrder-lift`
  embeds each level into the next, and `SurfaceDepth n` records exact
  same-binder depth with an embedding into `LiftedOrder (suc n)`.
* WF-1.89 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.RecursiveSurfaceOrder`): a direct
  recursive finite-closure candidate `_ <ᵇʳᶠ _` is now in place.
  Every derivation yields an exact depth witness `n` together with a
  `SurfaceDepth n` derivation, hence embeds into
  `LiftedOrder (suc n)` and is irreflexive.
* WF-1.895 (`Ordinal.Buchholz.RecursiveSurfaceBudget`): the recursive
  surface route now has an explicit well-founded budget carrier.
  A budgeted relation on `ℕ × BT` is well-founded by first-coordinate
  descent and still transports each step into the iterated wrapper
  tower.
* WF-2 (completed for mixed-head cases): bridge layer now includes
  `bpsi ν α <ᵇ bOmega μ` (under `ν ≤Ω μ`), left-summand bridges into
  additive terms (`<ᵇ-Ω+`, `<ᵇ-ψ+`), and additive-target bridges
  (`<ᵇ-+Ω`, `<ᵇ-+ψ`) with inversions.

Current Veblen-route status after this update:

* The comparison model now discharges the lifted same-index
  `ψ`-to-`+` comparison internally and instantiates the Veblen
  interface without deferred assumptions.
* `core-wf-from-comparison` now gives a concrete well-foundedness
  route for the current constructor core.
* This closure is deliberately scoped: it closes the route for the
  currently admitted constructors already present in
  `Ordinal.Buchholz.Order`.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.ExtendedOrder` now gives a second, larger closed
  relation on `BT` by pulling the comparison target back along
  `cmp-measure`; this relation includes the historical same-binder
  principles as lemmas and is transitive / well-founded.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.SurfaceOrder` now gives the first direct
  constructor-level bridge candidate: the same-binder shapes exist as
  constructors there, but still over current-core premises, and the
  closure argument runs by embedding into `_ <ᵇ⁺ _`.
* The attempted recursive surface lift is now explicitly blocked by a
  counterexample theorem in `Ordinal.Buchholz.SurfaceOrder`; the
  proved bridge remains `_ <ᵇˢ _`.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.LiftedExtendedOrder` now supplies the next
  well-founded wrapper `_ <ᵇ⁺¹ _`, showing that the surviving route is
  iterative wrapper lifting rather than a self-stability theorem for
  `_ <ᵇ⁺ _`.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.IteratedExtendedOrder` now packages that route at
  arbitrary finite depth: the wrapper construction is iterated over
  `ℕ`, and exact finite same-binder nesting is tracked by
  `SurfaceDepth`.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.RecursiveSurfaceOrder` now turns that indexed
  picture back into a single direct recursive candidate. The current
  theorem is per-derivation: any proof in `_ <ᵇʳᶠ _` has some finite
  depth and therefore lands in a suitable iterated wrapper.
* `Ordinal.Buchholz.RecursiveSurfaceBudget` now supplies the accepted
  recursion vehicle for the next theorem: once an explicit nat budget
  is carried, recursive-surface descent is well-founded and still
  lifts into the iterated wrappers.
* It does *not* yet promote the historically blocked shared-binder
  shapes into actual constructors of `_ <ᵇ _`; that remains the
  missing step between the current core and the full intended
  Buchholz order.
* `VeblenIdentityModel` and `VeblenProjectionMeasure` remain in-tree
  as explanatory scaffolds, not as live proof gaps.
* `VeblenComparisonTarget` now also exposes target-side transitivity
  lemmas (`≺P-trans`, `≺C-trans`) as preparation for any later
  recursive/shared-binder internalization of the full order.
* So there are two distinct follow-ups:
** editorial cleanup: decide which older intermediate scaffolds stay
   as explanatory stages and which can be demoted
** mathematical completion: internalize the shared-binder cases back
   into the real order package and then re-close inversion,
   transitivity, and well-foundedness for that enlarged order

Risk-gated bridge currently admitted:

* The original top-marker bridges remain as named specialised lemmas
  (`<ᵇ-+ω`, `<ᵇ-+ψω`) over the now-general additive-target bridges.

== The very first Agda proof: `C-monotone`

Before any ψ-value is defined, we must know the closure stages form a
coherent staged system: *increasing the stage index never removes
previously generable terms*. This is the structural theorem every
least-gap proof later leans on.

=== Why this is first

* `ψ(α)` is defined as the least ordinal *not already in `C(α)`*.
  Without a stable closure, that definition has no ground.
* Before attempting `psi-notin-C`, we need: closure is well-defined,
  closure inclusion between stages is monotone, closure references
  to earlier stages are admissible.
* Monotonicity is provable by a clean structural induction on the
  closure derivation, with no ordinal comparisons in sight.

=== Why ℕ stages, not OT stages

The first pass deliberately indexes closure stages by `ℕ`, not by
ordinal terms. This lets the induction be direct, teaches us the
closure-proof pattern, and avoids blocking on an OT ordering we have
not yet formalised. Generalisation to ordinal-indexed stages is the
point of E5.

=== Statement

----
C-monotone : ∀ {m n t} → m ≤ n → C m t → C n t
----

Proof by induction on the closure derivation:

* `c-zero`, `c-omega`: reconstruct at the larger stage.
* `c-plus`: recurse on the two subderivations.
* `c-psi k<m ck`: use `≤-trans k<m m≤n` to push the side condition
  up. This case is the real point — it forces the closure to be
  set up so the induction goes through.

=== Acceptance criteria

* `proofs/agda/Ordinal/Base.agda` compiles under `--safe --without-K`.
* `proofs/agda/Ordinal/Closure.agda` compiles and defines `C` and
  proves `C-monotone` *without postulates*.
* `C-monotone` is pinned by name in `Smoke.agda`.
* CI's full-suite and smoke jobs both pass.

== What is *not* yet claimed

* No ordinal semantics is asserted by these modules. `C`, `ψ`, and
  the Ω markers are pure syntax with evidence-bearing relations.
* Well-foundedness of the resulting notation system is a downstream
  milestone (part of E5 / E6), not a consequence of E1–E3.
* No connection to echo types is claimed until `EchoOrdinal.agda`
  lands. Do not rename load-bearing echo modules in anticipation.
