== The question

EI-2 (from `next-questions.adoc`):

[quote]
Does the integration recipe (decoration order + echo-indexed family
+ transport action) produce non-trivial content across other 2D
combinations, or did `RoleGraded` benefit from a fortunate
choreographic-and-grade pair?

The internal falsifier: if the trivial-case dominance is *uniform*
across all 2D combinations and gets *worse* in 3D, the recipe is
more like "vacuously holds in degenerate cases" than "load-bearing
integration content".

== Status

*EI-2 TERMINATED — negative verdict, via PATH B.*

The integration recipe with the existing five named axes does not
carry substantive simultaneous cross-axis content. The original
question — "does the recipe produce non-trivial content across
other 2D combinations, or did `RoleGraded` benefit from a fortunate
choreographic-and-grade pair?" — resolves negatively: the recipe
does not produce substantive simultaneous content in *any* of the
attempted axis pairings, including the self-pairing of the only
non-loss-only axis (`RoleRole.agda`).

Eight phases of investigation. Seven data points (sibling 2D
constructions, the 3D `RoleModeGrade` with three pairwise
commutations and a triple, and the self-pairing `RoleRole`). Across
all of them, every "non-trivial" cell carries one-axis-at-a-time
content; the load-bearing transport is always Choreo's
`client-to-server` at the c⊑s strict step, with the other axis
acting as identity. The non-loss-only criterion (formalised for
Choreo in `ChoreoInjective.agda`) is necessary but not sufficient
for substantive simultaneous interaction.

*Consequences for gate-1.* Distinctness against neighbour
frameworks rests on the *truncation argument* (`echo-not-prop`
family in `proofs/agda/examples/`) and the *2-cell argument*
(`Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair` and
`Sophisticated.meet→echo-intersection`), not on the integration
argument. Both arguments stand independently and are unaffected
by EI-2's negative resolution. The gate-1 claim has been narrowed
to reflect this across five doc locations (see "Documentation
cascade" below).

*Why PATH B (partial formalisation accepted as sufficient).* The
full 2D iff theorem is not formalisable in safe Agda without
postulates (decidable equality on decoration types, F-collapses
axioms, extensionality on cell actions). `RecipeTheorem.agda`
proves the per-axis halves; `RecipeNonTriviality.agda` proves the
concrete construction-by-construction halves; `RoleRole.agda`
provides the structural argument that even two non-loss-only
axes don't produce substantive simultaneous interaction.
Together with the seven data points, the empirical and structural
evidence is overwhelming. The formal generic theorem is documented
as future work; it is not required to settle the gate-1
distinctness question, since the negative finding *removes* the
integration recipe from the distinctness load.

The positive-termination route (extending the recipe to allow
coupled state across axes or multiple live positions per axis)
is parked for v0.2 and beyond. A new question will track this
if/when it becomes active. EI-2 itself is closed.

== Pattern observed

Across the three data points: the number of non-trivial cells
correlates with whether at least one axis has a *non-trivial
information-preserving transport* on at least one of its strict
(non-reflexive) steps.

* *Choreo* has `client-to-server` (the strict step `c⊑s`) — a
  witness-rearrangement that preserves cardinality. Non-loss-only.
* *Mode* has `weaken` (the strict step `linear≤affine`) — collapses
  to `EchoR ⊤ TrivialCert tt`. Loss-only.
* *Grade* has `collapse-to-residue` (the strict step `keep≤residue`)
  and several `tt`-collapses. All loss-only.

The constructions:

* Role × Grade: Role is non-loss-only, Grade is loss-only. Non-loss
  count: 1. Non-trivial cells: 1.
* Role × Mode: Role is non-loss-only, Mode is loss-only. Non-loss
  count: 1. Non-trivial cells: 1.
* Mode × Grade: Both are loss-only. Non-loss count: 0. Non-trivial
  cells: 0.

The correlation is exact across the three data points. Hypothesis:

[quote]
*Recipe non-triviality hypothesis:* the integration recipe
produces non-trivial commutation content iff at least one of the
two axes has a non-trivial information-preserving transport on at
least one strict step.

If this hypothesis holds in general, the recipe is *not* uniformly
non-trivial across all 2D combinations. It is non-trivial across
combinations satisfying a structural precondition that
Observation G in `gate-2-handoff.adoc` did not state explicitly.

== Step A: re-reading Observation G

Performed. Findings:

. Observation G does *not* explicitly require non-loss-only as a
  recipe precondition.
. The instances Observation G lists are *mixed*: Choreo is
  non-loss-only on `c⊑s`, while Graded and Linear are loss-only on
  their strict steps. Observation G groups them as instances of
  "the recipe" without distinguishing.
. Therefore: the recipe statement is silent on non-loss-only, and
  ModeGrade's 0-non-trivial result is not a violation of the
  *stated* recipe — it is a falsifier of an *unstated* uniformity
  expectation.

Conclusion: EI-2 has surfaced an implicit assumption in Observation
G that needs to be made explicit. Either:

* The recipe is *uniformly applicable* to any 2D combination of
  recipe-shaped axes (the strict reading), and ModeGrade falsifies
  this uniformity. Gate-1 weakens.
* The recipe is *applicable to non-loss-only-containing
  combinations*, and ModeGrade is outside the recipe's scope rather
  than a falsifier. Observation G needs amendment to state this
  precondition. Gate-1 narrows but holds.

== Step B: third data point with two non-loss-only axes

*Not feasible from existing modules.* Survey of the five named
axes:

* Choreographic — has `client-to-server`, `client-stability`. Both
  non-loss-only (information-preserving transports).
* Epistemic — `Knows` is parameterised by a *predicate*, not a
  finite enumeration. The recipe (in its Observation-G form) does
  not directly apply.
* Linear — `degradeMode linear≤affine = weaken`, loss-only.
* Graded — `degrade keep≤residue = collapse-to-residue`,
  loss-only. All other strict steps also loss-only.
* Tropical — score-indexed, no propositional decoration order on a
  finite type. Not recipe-shaped.

Of the five, only Choreo has natively non-trivial information-
preserving transports. The two-non-loss-only data point would
require either:

* Building a *new* axis (e.g., a "secure transport" axis with a
  non-trivial role-preserving action analogous to Choreo's
  `client-to-server`). New work, not a recipe test.
* Using two different Choreo transports on the same axis (e.g.,
  Choreo-via-`client-to-server` × Choreo-via-`client-stability`).
  This is a 1D × 1D self-product, not a true cross-axis test.

Neither is a clean answer.

== Step C: sharpen the non-loss-only criterion

In progress. The criterion in informal form:

[quote]
An axis (D, _≤_, F, t) is *non-loss-only* iff for at least one
strict step `d1 < d2`, the transport `t (d1≤d2) : F d1 → F d2`
is *injective on inhabitants*.

For the existing axes:

* Choreo: `client-to-server` is `map-square` applied to `swap`,
  which is a bijection on `Global = Bool × Bool`. `map-square`
  on a bijection is itself a bijection on the fiber type. So
  `client-to-server` is injective. *Non-loss-only confirmed.*
* Mode: `weaken = collapse-to-residue`. Collapses
  `Echo collapse tt` (multi-element) to `EchoR ⊤ TrivialCert tt`
  (singleton-or-near-singleton). Not injective. *Loss-only confirmed.*
* Grade: same as Mode for the corresponding strict step.

A formal Agda exhibit of `client-to-server-injective` would tighten
this argument. Open obligation:

[source,agda]
----
client-to-server-injective :
  ∀ {y : Bool} {e₁ e₂ : RoleEcho Client y} →
  client-to-server e₁ ≡ client-to-server e₂ →
  e₁ ≡ e₂
client-to-server-injective = ?
----

Once formalised, the criterion can be stated as a generic predicate
on axes and the recipe-non-triviality hypothesis can be sharpened
into a (provable or refutable) general theorem.

== Open obligations

These are the concrete next steps. None of them terminates EI-2 by
itself; each addresses a specific gap.

. *Formalise `client-to-server-injective`.* — *closed* in
  `proofs/agda/characteristic/ChoreoInjective.agda`. The lemma
  `client-to-server-injective-on-proj₁` proves the first-projection

  `client-to-server-preserves-distinction` is the formal
  multiplicity-preservation statement. Note: only first-projection
  injectivity is proved; full Σ-injectivity for fibers requires
  K-style reasoning, which `--without-K` precludes. For the EI-2

  ModeGrade). Non-loss-only axes contribute 1 non-trivial cell;
  loss-only axis pairs contribute 0; loss-only third axes do not

  adding loss-only axes does not produce qualitatively new patterns.

. *Write the recipe-non-triviality theorem.* — *partially closed*
  in `proofs/agda/characteristic/RecipeTheorem.agda`. Both
  directions of the iff are proved as per-axis lemmas:
+
--
* *Theorem 1* (`collapsing-loss-only`): every CollapsingAxis is
  loss-only at strict steps. Strict transports send everything to
  the same value in the collapsed codomain. This is the formal
  certificate behind ModeGrade's 0-of-18 result.
* *Theorem 2* (`carrier-NLO-distinguishes`): a CarrierAxis with
  NLO has a strict step preserving distinguishability. This is
  what makes RoleGraded's `(c⊑s, keep≤keep)` cell non-trivial.
--
+
The full 2D iff theorem is *not* formalised. Reasons documented in
the file header: the existing 2D constructions are hand-written,
each making pattern-match-on-data choices that don't lift to a
generic record framework; the natural generic 2D family
`F12 d_A d_B = (if d_B = top_B then F_A d_A else ⊤)` requires
decidable equality and collapse axioms as fields, and even then
the `with`-abstraction blocks reduction in dependent positions,
making proofs about specific cells require manual rewriting that
defeats the generic intent.
+
The conjectured full theorem is sketched as a Set₁-level
proposition without proof. The per-axis content of the
hypothesis is structurally certified by the two theorems above;
the global iff remains conjectured and supported by the seven
empirical data points in §<<data-points,Data points>>.
+
*Status of obligation 5:* partially closed. Full closure requires
formalising the conjectured 2D iff (technical work, structural
obstructions known and documented) or declaring partial closure
acceptable.

== Termination criteria for EI-2

EI-2 will be declared *terminated* if and only if one of the
following holds, with explicit formalised evidence:

* *Strict-reading falsifier sustained:* multiple data points show
  that the recipe is uniformly trivial across loss-only axis
  pairs, with no recipe-internal explanation. Gate-1's
  distinctness claim is weakened, this report records the
  weakening, the corresponding section in
  `gate-1-distinct-phenomenon.adoc` is updated.

* *Recipe refinement formalised:* the non-loss-only criterion is
  formalised, the recipe-non-triviality theorem is proved, and
  Observation G is amended to state the precondition explicitly.
  Gate-1's distinctness claim is narrowed to "the recipe is
  distinct *for axis pairs satisfying the non-loss-only criterion*".

* *Recipe-internal explanation found:* a non-trivial result emerges
  from a 2D loss-only-pair construction with a different framing
  (e.g., a different choice of family `F : D₁ → D₂ → Set`), and
  ModeGrade's 0-non-trivial result is shown to be artefactual.

Until one of these is satisfied, *EI-2 is open*. This document is
updated when new data points are added; the status line at the top
is the authoritative current state.
