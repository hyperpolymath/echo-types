== Audit of N1: `EchoGraded.degrade-via-join`

=== The lemma

[source,agda]
----
degrade-via-join :
  ŌłĆ {g1 g2 g}
  (p1 : g1 Ōēżg g) (p2 : g2 Ōēżg g) (e : GEcho g1) ŌåÆ
  degrade p1 e
  ŌēĪ degrade (Ōēżg-ŌŖög-univ p1 p2) (degrade (Ōēżg-ŌŖög-left g1 g2) e)
----

=== Reduction analysis

The lemma states a categorical join law on the loss-grade lattice:
degrading directly to a common upper bound `g` agrees with degrading
first to the join `g1 ŌŖög g2` and then onward via the universal arrow.

Substantive ingredients:

* `Ōēżg-ŌŖög-left`, `Ōēżg-ŌŖög-right`, `Ōēżg-ŌŖög-univ` ŌĆö the join structure.
  These exhibit `_ŌŖög_` as the categorical join in `_Ōēżg_`.
* `Ōēżg-prop` ŌĆö propositionality of the order.
* The `GEcho` family ŌĆö different types per grade (`Echo collapse tt` at
  keep, residue certificate at residue, `ŌŖż` at forget).

A na├»ve reduction: "this is a generic categorical join law on graded
families." Correct in shape, lossy in content. The lemma is *about* the
specific lattice modeling information loss.

[FALSIFIER]
====
Exhibit a non-loss-ordered three-element propositional join-semilattice
where the same lemma is also load-bearing for a structurally unrelated
reason. We currently have no such exhibition.
====

=== Verdict

*N1: SURVIVES.*

=== Reduction analysis

The third instance of the same recipe, on the *linearity-mode*
decoration. Substantive ingredients:

* `degradeMode-comp` ŌĆö associativity of mode weakening.
* `Ōēżm-prop` ŌĆö propositionality of the mode order. Two-element lattice
  (`linear Ōēżm affine`) with three constructors; same propositional
  structure as the role lattice and same general shape as the grade
  lattice.

  type variation as `GEcho`.
* The strict step `degradeMode linearŌēżaffine` is `weaken`, which
  factors through `collapse-to-residue` from `EchoResidue`. This is
  *specific* content ŌĆö the weakening is not the identity, and it has
  the irreversibility witness `no-section-collapse-to-residue`
  attached.

two-element propositional order." Correct in shape, lossy in content.
The lemma is about the specific propositional order on linearity
modes, with the strict weakening `weaken` having concrete
non-recoverability content.

[FALSIFIER]

role-lattice and `weaken` substituted for `client-to-server`:

is not unique to a single decoration.

== New construction: `characteristic.RoleGraded` (closes handoff Observation E)

The Revision-3 handoff document recorded the following open
recommendation in Observation E:

[quote]
A genuine integration theorem ŌĆö one in which a single Echo carries
both a role/choreographic decoration and a graded decoration, and the
two conjuncts share data ŌĆö is not currently in the repository.
Building one is open work.

This revision closes that recommendation.

=== The construction

`proofs/agda/characteristic/RoleGraded.agda` defines:

* `RoleGEcho : Role ŌåÆ Grade ŌåÆ Set` ŌĆö a 2D family that varies
  non-trivially in both axes. At keep grade,
  `RoleGEcho r keep = RoleEcho r true`; at residue and forget grades,
  `ŌŖż`.
* `applyRole : (r1 ŌŖæc r2) ŌåÆ RoleGEcho r1 g ŌåÆ RoleGEcho r2 g` ŌĆö role
  transport. At keep grade it does real work
  (`client-to-server` for `cŌŖæs`); past keep, identity on `ŌŖż`.
* `applyGrade : (g1 Ōēżg g2) ŌåÆ RoleGEcho r g1 ŌåÆ RoleGEcho r g2` ŌĆö grade
  degradation, parametric in the role.

=== The headline theorem

[source,agda]
----
choreo-grade-commute :
  ŌłĆ {r1 r2 g1 g2}
  (rp : r1 ŌŖæc r2) (gp : g1 Ōēżg g2)
  (e : RoleGEcho r1 g1) ŌåÆ
  applyGrade gp (applyRole rp e) ŌēĪ applyRole rp (applyGrade gp e)
----

The two actions commute: applying them in either order on a
RoleGEcho yields the same result. Eighteen cases, each closing by
`refl` after pattern matching. The non-trivial case is
`(cŌŖæs, keepŌēżkeep)` where both sides reduce to `client-to-server e`.

A combined two-decoration action `applyBoth` is also provided, with
its agreement under either factoring proved as a corollary.

=== Why this passes the Observation-E tests

[cols="1,4", options="header"]
|===
| Test | Verdict
| 1. Single 2D family on which both decorations act. | Pass: `RoleGEcho` varies in both `Role` and `Grade`.
| 2. Single combined action consuming both decoration witnesses. | Pass: `applyBoth rp gp e` consumes both `rp` and `gp`.
| 3. Same-data test: both sides of the conclusion mention both decoration witnesses. | Pass: both `rp` and `gp` appear on both sides of `choreo-grade-commute`.
|===

The withdrawn `knowledge-and-controlled-degradation` nominee failed
test 3 (its conjuncts were over different functions and shared no
data). `choreo-grade-commute` passes all three.

=== Honest qualification: protocol pass, narrow content (EI-2)

The three Observation-E tests are *protocol tests*: they ask whether
the construction has the right shape (single 2D family, single
combined action, same-data conclusion). `RoleGraded` passes by
construction.

What the protocol tests do *not* ask: how much of the 2D family's
content is non-trivial. Of `choreo-grade-commute`'s 18 cases (3
role constructors ├Ś 6 grade constructors), exactly one carries
non-trivial cross-decoration content (`(cŌŖæs, keepŌēżkeep)`, reducing
to `client-to-server e`). The other 17 close trivially because
residue/forget grades collapse `RoleGEcho` to `ŌŖż` (12 cases) or
because the role-reachability constructors are reflexive (5 cases).
The 2D family is asymmetric ŌĆö the role parameter is only live at
keep grade.

This is a *protocol-correct* closure of EI-1. It is *substantively
narrow*. The follow-up question ŌĆö is the recipe robust across
other 2D combinations (Role ├Ś Mode, Mode ├Ś Grade, etc.), or did
`RoleGraded` benefit from a fortunate axis pair? ŌĆö is tracked as
EI-2 in `docs/next-questions.adoc`. EI-2 is a sharpening question,
not a survival question; its negative resolution would weaken
EI-1's closure to "closed-but-narrow" but would not invalidate it.

Do not relabel EI-1 as open. The protocol pass is the protocol
pass. EI-2 records what's still substantively open without moving
the goalposts.

=== Withdrawn ŌĆö `EchoCharacteristic.visible-constraint`

==== The lemma

[source,agda]
----
visible : State ŌåÆ Bool       -- State = Bool ├Ś Bool
visible = projŌéü

visible-constraint :
  ŌłĆ {y : Bool} (e : Echo visible y) ŌåÆ projŌéü (projŌéü e) ŌēĪ y
visible-constraint (_ , p) = p
----

==== Why withdrawn

`visible := projŌéü` definitionally, so `Echo visible y` is
`╬Ż State (╬╗ s ŌåÆ projŌéü s ŌēĪ y)`. The standard `projŌéé` on this ╬Ż has the
same type as `visible-constraint`, and both compute by `refl` on every
inhabitant. The reduction `visible-constraint ŌēĪ projŌéé` is formalised
in `proofs/agda/characteristic/VisibleConstraintAudit.agda`.

Per the Gate 2 test, "fiber lemma X with renamed components does not
count." `visible-constraint` is `projŌéé` with renamed components.

==== Why retained in EchoCharacteristic

The lemma has expository value as a worked-example illustration. The
withdrawal is from *the Gate 2 nominee list only*.

=== Withdrawn ŌĆö `EchoIntegration.knowledge-and-controlled-degradation`

==== The theorem

[source,agda]
----
knowledge-and-controlled-degradation :
  ŌłĆ {y : Bool} {P : Global ŌåÆ Set} ŌåÆ
  (ŌłĆ g ŌåÆ P g ŌåÆ P (scramble-server g)) ŌåÆ
  Knows Client P y ŌåÆ
  (ŌłĆ e ŌåÆ P (projŌéü (client-stability e)))
  ├Ś (degrade keepŌēżresidue echo-true ŌēĪ degrade keepŌēżresidue echo-false)
----

==== Why withdrawn (sharpened falsifier)

The two conjuncts are over different functions: conjunct 1 over
`obs Client : Global ŌåÆ Bool`; conjunct 2 over `collapse : Bool ŌåÆ ŌŖż`.
The proof closes by literally pairing the two facts. The hypotheses
flow only into conjunct 1.

The formal exhibit is in
`proofs/agda/characteristic/IntegrationAudit.agda`:

* `split-knowledge`, `split-merging` ŌĆö the two conjuncts in isolation.
* `N3-is-product : knowledge-and-controlled-degradation inv k ŌēĪ
  (split-knowledge inv k , split-merging)`, by `refl`.
* `split-merging-uses-no-hypotheses` ŌĆö confirming no shared data.

The "integration" is `_├Ś_` with no logical link. The named theorem
does not integrate; it pairs.

==== Why retained in EchoIntegration

The theorem stands as a true proposition. It marks the slot where a
genuine integration theorem would live. As of Revision 4, that slot
is filled by `characteristic.RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`; the
withdrawn lemma can be left in place as a historical signpost.
