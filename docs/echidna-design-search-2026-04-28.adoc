= Echidna design-search findings ‚Äî Phase 1.3 + unbudgeted wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý_
:date: 2026-04-28

This document captures the output of running Echidna's new
`design search` strategy against two open targets in the Buchholz /
Brouwer programme. It is a *recommendation handoff*, not a proof ‚Äî
the Agda translation is the next session's work.

== Background

Two open milestones in `docs/buchholz-plan.adoc`:

* **Phase 1.3** ‚Äî monotonicity lemmas for `Ordinal.Brouwer.Arithmetic`
  (`osuc-mono-‚â§`, `‚äï-mono-‚â§-right`). Blocked because the current
  `data _‚â§_` axiomatisation makes `osuc-mono-‚â§` non-trivial in the
  `‚â§-lim` case.
* **Unbudgeted `wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý_`** ‚Äî `Ordinal.Buchholz.RecursiveSurfaceBudget`
  carries a `BudgetedBT = ‚Ñï √ó BT` budget alongside each derivation;
  the goal is `WellFounded _<·µá ≥·∂Ý_` directly without the budget.

These were attacked using a new Echidna capability stack (built in
this session):

. `echidna corpus ingest` ‚Äî structural index of every named
  declaration in `proofs/agda/Ordinal/**.agda`, plus dependency DAG
  and axiom-hazard flags.
. `echidna design search <problem>` ‚Äî simulated annealing over
  *design space* (axiomatisation shapes / rank shapes), not tactic
  space. Energy is symbolic, computed from the corpus + heuristic
  predicates.
. `echidna design swarm <problem>` ‚Äî N parallel SA agents with
  cross-pollination through a coordinator. Used to validate that the
  single-chain SA result was robust.

== Phase 1.3 ‚Äî recommended `_‚â§_` redesign

=== What Echidna found

`echidna design search brouwer-leq --top 12 --iterations 2000`
agrees across all top candidates: switch `_‚â§_` to a *recursive*
definition.

Energy `[0, 0, 1, 0]` ‚Äî zero monotonicity blockers, zero K-elim
hazards, single seed constructor, recursive style.

Data-style alternatives (with `LimBelow` added) score `[2, 0, 4, 1]`
or worse; my hand-trace of `osuc-mono-‚â§` under `data + LimBelow`
confirms the SA's verdict ‚Äî in the `‚â§-lim n q` case, neither
`‚â§-suc ‚Üí ‚â§-lim k` nor `LimBelow` recovers a path from
`q : x ‚â§ f n` to `osuc x ‚â§ osuc (olim f)`. The recursive form makes
`osuc-mono-‚â§ p = p` because the equation
`osuc Œ± ‚â§ osuc Œ≤ = Œ± ‚â§ Œ≤` reduces the goal definitionally.

=== Recommended axiomatisation

Sketched (proof bodies omitted ‚Äî see "Proof obligations" below):

[source,agda]
----
_‚â§_ : Ord ‚Üí Ord ‚Üí Set
oz      ‚â§ _        = ‚ä§
osuc Œ±  ‚â§ oz       = ‚ä•
osuc Œ±  ‚â§ osuc Œ≤   = Œ± ‚â§ Œ≤
osuc Œ±  ‚â§ olim f   = Œ£ ‚Ñï Œª n ‚Üí osuc Œ± ‚â§ f n
olim f  ‚â§ Œ≤        = ‚àÄ n ‚Üí f n ‚â§ Œ≤
----

=== Proof obligations under this axiomatisation

. `‚â§-refl : ‚àÄ {Œ±} ‚Üí Œ± ‚â§ Œ±` ‚Äî structural induction on `Œ±`.
. `‚â§-step : ‚àÄ {Œ± Œ≤} ‚Üí Œ± ‚â§ Œ≤ ‚Üí Œ± ‚â§ osuc Œ≤` ‚Äî non-trivial; case-splits on
  both arguments.
. `‚â§-trans : ‚àÄ {Œ± Œ≤ Œ≥} ‚Üí Œ± ‚â§ Œ≤ ‚Üí Œ≤ ‚â§ Œ≥ ‚Üí Œ± ‚â§ Œ≥` ‚Äî induct on `Œ≥` first
  (most reductions branch on the right argument).
. `f-in-lim : ‚àÄ f n ‚Üí f n ‚â§ olim f` ‚Äî pattern on `f n`; for the
  successor case, supply `Œ£` witness `(n , ‚â§-refl)`.
. `osuc-mono-‚â§ p = p` ‚Äî *identity* by definitional reduction. **The
  whole point of the redesign.**
. `‚äï-mono-‚â§-right : ‚àÄ {Œ±} {x y} ‚Üí x ‚â§ y ‚Üí Œ± ‚äï x ‚â§ Œ± ‚äï y` ‚Äî structural
  on `Œ±` (since `_‚äï_` is left-recursive).
. `wf-< : WellFounded _<_` ‚Äî needs re-proof under the recursive
  definition. The structural-induction proof in the current
  `Brouwer.agda` uses `Acc _<_` constructed from
  `pred-of-osuc`/`pred-of-olim`; adapt those to the recursive `_‚â§_`.

=== Risks I noticed during hand-tracing

* The `‚â§-step` lemma ‚Äî currently a single line `‚â§-step = ‚â§-suc` ‚Äî is
  non-trivial under recursive `_‚â§_`. Specifically the case
  `Œ± = osuc Œ±', Œ≤ = olim f` requires `Œ±' ‚â§ osuc (olim f)`, which
  case-splits to `Œ£ n. osuc Œ±' ‚â§ f n` ‚Äî needs careful witness picking.
* `pred-of-olim` ‚Äî currently uses pattern match on `‚â§-lim n q`; under
  recursive `_‚â§_`, the relation isn't pattern-matchable, so this
  becomes a structural fact about `Œ£`-elimination.

These don't block the redesign ‚Äî they're well-trodden constructive
ordinal proofs in literature ‚Äî but they represent real proof work,
not a one-line refactor.

== Unbudgeted `wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý_` ‚Äî recommended rank shape

=== What Echidna found

`echidna design search buchholz-rank --top 5` and
`echidna design swarm buchholz-rank --agents 4` both converge on the
same minimal-blocker shape with energy `[3, 0, 1]`:

[source,agda]
----
rank : BT ‚Üí Ord
rank bzero        = oz
rank (bOmega ŒΩ)   = œâ-rank ŒΩ
rank (bplus x y)  = rank x ‚äï rank y
rank (bpsi ŒΩ Œ±)   = psi-rank ŒΩ (rank Œ±)
----

The three blockers are the three `_<·µá ≥·∂Ý_` constructor cases of
`rank-mono`. Two of them (`<·µá ≥·∂Ý-œàŒ±`, `<·µá ≥·∂Ý-+2`) reduce to the same
new lemma `‚äï-mono-<-right` (a Phase-1.3 deliverable). The third
(`<·µá ≥·∂Ý-core`) reduces to Phase-2.2's `rank-mono-<·µá` for the base
Buchholz order.

=== Closing the goal

Once Phase-1.3 monotonicity and Phase-2.2 rank-mono land:

[source,agda]
----
rank-mono : ‚àÄ {x y} ‚Üí x <·µá ≥·∂Ý y ‚Üí rank x < rank y
rank-mono (<·µá ≥·∂Ý-core p) = rank-mono-<·µá p
rank-mono (<·µá ≥·∂Ý-œàŒ±   p) = -- œà-strict-mono via osuc-strict-mono
                          --   on (œâ-rank ŒΩ ‚äï rank Œ±)
                          --   ‚üπ ‚äï-mono-<-right
rank-mono (<·µá ≥·∂Ý-+2   p) = ‚äï-mono-<-right p

wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý : WellFounded _<·µá ≥·∂Ý_
wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý = Subrelation.wellFounded rank-mono
            (InverseImage.wellFounded rank wf-<)
----

(`Subrelation`, `InverseImage` from `Induction.WellFounded`. `wf-<`
is the existing Brouwer well-foundedness ‚Äî itself unchanged by the
Phase-1.3 redesign so long as the redesign keeps it well-founded.)

== Proof-obligation graph

The two milestones share dependencies. In the order I'd attack them:

. **`osuc-mono-<` (strict version) and `‚äï-mono-<-right`** in
  `Ordinal.Brouwer` + `Ordinal.Brouwer.Arithmetic`. Phase-1.3
  deliverables. Recursive `_‚â§_` makes `osuc-mono-<` trivial; `‚äï-mono-<-
  right` is structural on `Œ±`.
. **Re-prove `wf-<` under the recursive `_‚â§_`.** Structural on `Ord`
  with adapted `pred-of-osuc` / `pred-of-olim`.
. **`rank-mono-<·µá : x <·µá y ‚Üí rank x < rank y`** in a new
  `Ordinal.Buchholz.Rank` module. Phase-2.2 deliverable. Constructor-
  by-constructor induction over the 13-shape admitted core of `_<·µá_`.
. **Drop `RecursiveSurfaceBudget`** in favour of direct
  `wf-<·µá ≥·∂Ý : WellFounded _<·µá ≥·∂Ý_` via the `Subrelation` chain. The
  budget infrastructure becomes vestigial.

== SA energy assumptions (for sanity-checking)

The SA's energy is symbolic, not from running Agda. Two predicates
might be wrong; spot-check before fully trusting the recommendation:

* **`mono_osuc_ok` (in `learning/design_search.rs`)**: claims that
  `_‚â§_` is `Recursive` *or* contains `‚â§-cong-suc` ‚üπ `osuc-mono-‚â§`
  is trivial. I verified by hand for the `Recursive` case. The
  `‚â§-cong-suc` claim is also straightforward (apply the constructor).
  No data-style + `LimBelow` path is recognised ‚Äî confirmed by hand
  trace.
* **`blockers` (in `learning/buchholz_rank.rs`)**: claims that
  `<·µá ≥·∂Ý-+2` always needs `‚äï-mono-<-right`, regardless of whether
  `bplus` rank is `LeftSum` or `OsucSum`. The `OsucSum` case
  technically needs `osuc-strict-mono ‚àò ‚äï-mono-<-right` ‚Äî the outer
  `osuc` is still strict-monotonic, so the dependency reduces to
  `‚äï-mono-<-right` either way. Confirmed.
