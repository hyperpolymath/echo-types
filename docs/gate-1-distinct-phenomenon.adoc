= Gate #1: distinct phenomenon
:toc: macro
:source-highlighter: rouge

This document carries the falsifiable argument for Gate #1 of the
echo-types identity claim, per the README:

[quote]
____
Distinct phenomenon: structured loss under non-injective computation.
____

The gate is treated as established only if the argument here survives
the falsification tests in <<falsification>>. It is retracted in
writing if any test succeeds.

toc::[]

== The honest problem

The construction `Echo f y := Σ (x : A) , (f x ≡ y)` is the homotopy
fiber of `f` at `y`. There is no syntactic novelty. Distinctness
therefore cannot be claimed at the level of the construction. It can
only be claimed at the level of *role*, *composition*, and
*theorems-that-do-not-restate*.

This is the bar Gate #1 actually has to clear. Any informed reviewer
will press on it first, and any defence that conflates "Echo is well-
founded" with "Echo is distinct" should be rejected.

== Neighbouring theories

Per-neighbour distinctness comparisons live in `docs/adjacency/`.
Each neighbour gets its own file with an explicit (narrow)
distinctness claim, identification of which structural argument
(truncation, 2-cell, multiplicity) is load-bearing, formal exhibit
pointers, and explicit retraction conditions.

The two argument families that emerged from working through the
neighbours individually are framed in `docs/adjacency/README.adoc`.
Briefly:

* *Truncation arguments* (refinement, IFC, provenance, quotients):
  echo wins by carrying type-data where the neighbour carries
  propositional or single-witness content. Formal certificate is
  the family of `echo-not-prop` lemmas in
  `proofs/agda/examples/`.
* *2-cell arguments* (quotients, Galois): echo wins because the
  natural 2-cell in the neighbour's framework is
  Σ-over-preimages-shaped. Formal certificates are
  `EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair` and
  `EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated.meet→echo-intersection`.

Two neighbours not in the original five-neighbour list are now
also covered:

* *Modal type theories* (QTT, Reed-Pfenning, etc.) — co-extensive
  with echo on bare echo in QTT-class systems; recipe-level
  distinctness is an open question. See
  `docs/adjacency/modal-type-theories.adoc`.
* *HoTT homotopy fibers* — echo IS the homotopy fiber; the win
  is over the propositional truncation modality, not over fibers
  themselves. See `docs/adjacency/hott-fibers.adoc`.

The honest summary: gate #1 has a strong consolidated argument
plus formalised obstructions against the two strongest neighbours
(quotients, Galois) plus formalised truncation wins on three
domains (tropical, epistemic, linear). The remaining neighbours
have framed but only-partially-formalised distinctness claims;
see each file for what's done and what's open.

== Where distinctness can live

Not in the object. In the *integration of structured-loss across
orthogonal axes*.

The bridge modules in `proofs/agda/` already span:

* choreographic — role projection (`EchoChoreo`)
* epistemic — indistinguishability and knowledge (`EchoEpistemic`)
* affine/linear — strict weakening to residues (`EchoLinear`)
* graded — compositional degradation (`EchoGraded`)
* tropical — argmin-style witnesses (`EchoTropical`)

None of the five neighbour traditions span this breadth natively.
Galois lives in lattices. IFC lives in security policies. Quotients
are usually fixed (no native graded-chain construction). Refinements
have no native grading or choreography. Provenance has no tropical
version.

The integration theorem now in the repository is
`characteristic.RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`: a single theorem
on a 2D family `RoleGEcho : Role → Grade → Set` carrying both a
choreographic role decoration and a graded loss decoration, with
two independent actions (`applyRole`, `applyGrade`) that commute.
Both decoration witnesses appear on both sides of the equation,
satisfying the same-data test that the previous nominee
(`EchoIntegration.knowledge-and-controlled-degradation`, which gate-2's
audit exhibited as a literal product of disjoint facts —
see `proofs/agda/characteristic/IntegrationAudit.agda`) failed.

This combination is not native to any one neighbour tradition. Each
neighbour would have to import machinery from at least two others to
express it.

[#claim]
=== The Gate #1 claim

[quote]
____
Echo types are not novel as a construction. Distinctness against
neighbouring frameworks rests on two structural arguments:

. *Truncation argument.* For non-injective `f` with multiple
  preimages of `y`, `Echo f y` is constructively not a mere
  proposition (`echo-not-prop` family in
  `proofs/agda/examples/`). Neighbour theories that propositionally
  truncate the witness type (preimage subset, equivalence class,
  IFC equivalence relation, propositional-predicate refinement
  type) lose what echo retains.

. *2-cell argument.* The natural 2-cells in neighbour frameworks
  (quotient equalizers, Galois meets) are structurally
  Σ-over-preimages-shaped — i.e., already echo-shaped. Formal
  certificates in
  `proofs/agda/EchoVsQuotient.Sophisticated.equalizer→echo-pair`
  and
  `proofs/agda/EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated.meet→echo-intersection`,
  with diagonal correspondence via
  `meet-on-diagonal→equalizer`.

The earlier framing — distinctness as the *coherent integration
of structured loss across five orthogonal axes simultaneously* —
is *narrower* than originally stated. The EI-2 investigation
(`docs/EI2_REPORT.adoc`, plus the sibling constructions in
`proofs/agda/characteristic/`) establishes that the recipe
(decoration order + echo-indexed family + transport action)
exhibits one-axis-at-a-time cross-axis content embedded in 2D
families, not substantive simultaneous cross-axis interaction.
This holds across all attempted axis pairings, including the
self-pairing of the only non-loss-only axis (`RoleRole.agda`).

The integration argument therefore *does not* carry the
distinctness load. The truncation and 2-cell arguments do, and
they stand independently. The integration recipe remains useful as
organising vocabulary for fiber-shaped reasoning across multiple
axes; it is not the locus of distinctness.
____

This is the claim that has to survive falsification.

[#falsification]
== Falsification tests

Three concrete tests. Each carries an explicit retraction condition.
If any one test succeeds against echo types, Gate #1 is retracted or
substantially weakened.

=== T1 — Galois reduction

Take the characteristic-family commutation theorem (currently `RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`). Rewrite it in pure
abstract-interpretation terms — Galois connection between concrete
and abstract domains, with grading expressed as a chain of
approximations.

PASSES (gate falsified) if::
The rewrite preserves all proof-relevant content with no machinery
imported from outside abstract interpretation.

FAILS (gate supported) if::
The rewrite cannot express the cross-axis composition without
importing graded or choreographic primitives that AI does not
natively contain.

Attempt scaffolded in `proofs/agda/EchoVsGalois.agda`.

=== T2 — Quotient reduction

Same theorem, restated as a statement about the quotient
`B = A / ker f` plus a quotient-of-quotients construction for the
graded chain.

PASSES (gate falsified) if::
The restatement goes through cleanly using only quotient operations.

FAILS (gate supported) if::
The graded composition or choreographic action does not commute with
the quotient stacking, leaving a residue that quotient theory cannot
express.

Attempt scaffolded in `proofs/agda/EchoVsQuotient.agda`.

=== T3 — Informativeness collapse

Show that `EchoEpistemic`'s indistinguishability-and-knowledge
results are derivable from `EchoLinear` plus a forgetful functor —
i.e., that the epistemic axis is not pulling its weight.

PASSES (gate weakened) if::
Derivation goes through. The "five axes" claim shrinks to four or
fewer; the integration argument weakens correspondingly.

FAILS (gate supported) if::
Derivation fails at a specific point where epistemic structure
carries content not present in linear structure.

This test is currently unstubbed; see <<open-questions>>.

== Status of attempts

T1 (Galois)::
Scaffolded in `proofs/agda/EchoVsGalois.agda`. The basic reduction
holds for the foundation module. The non-monotone-grade obstruction
is now *witnessed* by the `Sophisticated` submodule, which attacks
the natural AI 2-cell candidate — the MEET in the abstraction
lattice — and shows its data is Σ-over-preimages with a paired
equality predicate (i.e., the intersection of two Echo types).
+
The Galois argument runs through a different lattice operation
(meet of abstractions) than the quotient argument (equalizer of
functions), unfolding to a different but structurally equivalent
shape. The two arguments factor through the same underlying type
at the diagonal: `meet-on-diagonal→equalizer` shows the AI meet
specialises to the quotient equalizer when the output values
coincide.
+
The combined T1+T2 conclusion: the obstruction is AXIS-PAIR-
SPECIFIC, not an artifact of any one neighbour theory's framework.
A reviewer who wants to falsify gate #1 must find a 2-cell
construction that lives outside both lattice traditions.

T2 (Quotient)::
Scaffolded in `proofs/agda/EchoVsQuotient.agda`. Foundation reduces
cleanly. The naive-quotient-stacking obstruction is now *witnessed*
by the `Miniature` submodule: a concrete two-element-type setting
(A = Bool × Bool, one grade step, one role projection) where role
and grade demonstrably do not commute, and their kernel relations
differ at a specific witness pair. Naive quotient stacking is
therefore proved to fail. The 2-categorical encoding — the most
plausible sophisticated quotient construction — is attacked in the
`Sophisticated` submodule, which establishes:
+
--
* Kernels of the two compositions can be made *incomparable* in
  a non-degenerate two-bit setting (parity vs. first-bit), so a
  true 2-cell is needed rather than a refinement map.
* The natural 2-cell (StrictComparison = equalizer of the two
  compositions) has the shape of an Echo type by definition.
* Any StrictComparison witness yields a pair of Echo witnesses
  over a shared output value (`equalizer→echo-pair`).
--
+
The 2-categorical encoding therefore cannot avoid importing echo
machinery at the level of its 2-cell data. Remaining T2 sub-cases
are listed in Q1.6.

T3 (Informativeness collapse)::
No scaffold yet. Awaiting stable signatures from `EchoEpistemic` and
`EchoLinear` before attempting. Status: open obligation.

[#open-questions]
== Open questions

These should be promoted into `docs/next-questions.adoc` when the
gate-1 work is integrated. They are written here in the form they
should appear there.

Q1.1::
Does there exist a Galois-theoretic construction that expresses
`RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute` without importing graded or
choreographic primitives? If yes, Gate #1 is falsified by T1.

Q1.2::
Does quotient stacking commute with role projection in enough cases
to express the choreographic-graded composition? If yes, Gate #1 is
falsified by T2.

Q1.3::
Is `EchoEpistemic.knowledge-preservation` derivable from
`EchoLinear.weakening` plus a forgetful functor? If yes, Gate #1 is
weakened by T3.

Q1.4::
Are there axes beyond the current five (choreo, epistemic, linear,
graded, tropical) that should be in the integration picture? Each
candidate axis must come with its own characteristic theorem
(Gate #2) and canonical example (Gate #3) before being adopted.

Q1.5 [partial answer]::
The smallest counter-example for naive quotient stacking has been
constructed: see `proofs/agda/EchoVsQuotient.agda` § `Miniature`.
+
The full T2 obligation is now decomposed into three claims:
+
--
(C1) Naive quotient stacking fails — *proved* in `Miniature` via
`kernel-disagreement`.

(C2) Sophisticated quotient constructions (joins of kernels,
2-categorical structures over kernels, indexed families of quotients
with coherence laws, ...) can in principle be attempted — *true,
requires survey*.

(C3) Every sophisticated quotient construction either fails on
the characteristic-family commutation theorem (currently `RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`), or imports machinery equivalent to
echo types (Σ over preimages with cross-axis transport) — *open*.
This is the real claim of gate #1; (C1) is necessary but not
sufficient evidence. Partial closure: the 2-categorical encoding
case is attacked in `Sophisticated`, with the structural argument
running through the equalizer characterisation. Remaining sub-cases
listed in Q1.6.
--

Q1.6::
C3 sub-cases remaining for full closure. Each is a separate
falsification sub-test, attackable independently. From most to
least concerning:
+
--
* (S-1) *Joins of kernels.* — *closed* in
  `Sophisticated.Joins`. The join is defined inductively as the
  smallest equivalence relation containing both kernels;
  `join-universal` proves every pair of elements is identified
  (via spanning lemma `all-joined-to-TT`, which establishes that
  every element joins to (true, true) in at most two steps).
  Quotients by the join therefore collapse A to a singleton and
  lose all integration content.

* (S-2) *Indexed families of quotients with coherence laws.* —
  *closed* in `Sophisticated.IndexedFamilies`. Two-element index
  (Grade vs Role); `CoherenceLaw Grade Role` unfolds
  definitionally to `StrictComparison`, so the identity gives a
  bijection. `s-2-reduction` runs the conversion through
  `equalizer→echo-pair`, inheriting the structural identification
  with Σ-over-preimages. Generalisation to larger indices is
  mechanical: the same argument applies pointwise to each pair
  of parallel 1-cells in the index.

* (S-3) *Strict 2-categories with primitive 2-cells.* 2-cells
  given axiomatically rather than constructed from underlying
  data. Such 2-cells carry no specific information and cannot
  capture the characteristic-family commutation theorem's specific
  content. Status: short
  argument in module comments.

* (S-4) *Higher inductive types / HoTT.* Admit echo-shaped 2-
  cells natively, but at the cost of importing the entire HoTT
  framework. By the conservative reading of "pure quotient
  theory", this counts as importing machinery far in excess of
  echo types. Gate #1 supported by reductio: any sufficiently
  expressive sophisticated encoding either uses Σ-over-preimages
  directly or imports HoTT, which subsumes Σ-over-preimages.

If any of (S-1)–(S-4) admits a faithful encoding of
the characteristic-family commutation theorem (currently `RoleGraded.choreo-grade-commute`) without importing echo machinery,
gate #1 is falsified by that sub-case. Each requires its own
focused attempt.
--

Q1.7 [partial answer]::
Does the Galois reduction (T1) admit a 2-cell construction
escaping the Σ-over-preimages shape?
+
--
The natural AI 2-cell candidate is the MEET in the abstraction
lattice. `EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated.meet→echo-intersection` shows
the meet's data unpacks directly into two Echo witnesses sharing
a preimage. The meet operation in AI's lattice has the same
Σ-over-preimages shape as the quotient equalizer, just with a
paired predicate instead of a single one.

The diagonal correspondence (`meet-on-diagonal→equalizer`)
establishes that the AI meet specialises to the quotient
equalizer when output values coincide; off the diagonal, the
meet is strictly more general but structurally identical.

Remaining open: the abstraction-lattice analog of S-1 (lattice
operations degenerate), formally an instance of the
`Joins.join-universal` argument in opposite direction; and
higher-order Galois constructions (functor-category insertions,
Galois-of-Galois) which reduce structurally to the indexed-
families case (S-2). Both omitted to avoid duplication; arguments
sketched in `EchoVsGalois.Sophisticated`'s status block.
--

== Distinctness commitment

If T1, T2, or T3 succeeds, the README's identity claim is retracted
or weakened correspondingly, and this document is updated to record
the result. The commitment is not "we will explain why the test
doesn't really apply"; it is "we will retract".

The repo's identity is gated. That is the point of having gates.
