This document sets out the path by which "echo types" can acquire
*recognised type-theoretic standing*. It is deliberately separate from
`roadmap.adoc` (work partitioned by lane) and `roadmap-gates.adoc`
(the falsifiable identity claim). This document answers a different
question: _given_ the gated identity claim, what makes a construction
like this *established* in the sense that linear, dependent, or graded
modal types are established — and what specifically must this repository
ship to get there.

== The honest type-theoretic verdict

`Echo f y := Σ (x : A) , (f x ≡ y)` is *definitionally* the homotopy
fibre `fib f y` (HoTT book Def. 4.2.4). This fact governs the entire
strategy and is owned, not hedged:

* *"Linear" and "dependent" are the wrong target — comparing to them is
  a category error.* Dependent types are a _formation_ discipline
  (types depend on terms; semantics = LCCC / CwF). Linear and
  substructural types are a _structural-rule_ discipline
  (contraction/weakening restricted; semantics = symmetric monoidal
  closed categories / Benton's LNL). Both change the _judgmental_
  apparatus of the theory. Echo introduces no new judgment, context
  structure, or formation rule — it is a derived Σ-type. Shipping
  bespoke "echo introduction/elimination rules" dressed as primitive
  would be the fastest possible way to lose every informed reader.

* *The strength that befits echo is a loss-graded reindexing
  modality — motivated by, but not an instance of, the coeffect /
  quantitative lineage* (Petriček–Orchard–Mycroft coeffects;
  Brunel–Gaboardi–Mazza–Zdancewic; Atkey's Quantitative Type Theory;
  Orchard–Liepelt–Eades / Granule; Gaboardi et al. graded modal
  types). [RETRACTED READING: this was originally "a graded comonadic
  modality… proved as a graded comonad of structured loss". It is
  *not* a graded comonad — no nested `D_r D_s` is formed. The repo's
  code has a thin-poset reindexing action: `EchoGraded`'s loss-grade
  lattice + monotone `degrade`, `EchoLinear`'s two-mode degradation,
  `EchoCategorical`'s slice/fibration functor laws.]

[#thesis]
.The establishment thesis (narrowed 2026-05-18)
****
Echo is the homotopy fibre, equipped with a loss-grade lattice,
carrying a *definitionally-grounded loss-graded reindexing modality*
(a thin-poset action — *not* a graded comonad). Its standing comes
not from being a new object but from being *exactly characterised and
honestly bounded*: a definitional Σ-identity; a funext-relative
pointwise mediator property; coherence equations that provably reduce
to thinness of the grade order; a separating model that locates that
reduction; a carrier-parametric abstraction; a postulate-free build;
and a falsifiable retraction discipline of which the 2026-05-18
reframing is itself an instance. _Original wording: "forming a
definitionally-grounded graded comonad of structured loss… a
universal property, multiple models, an irreducibility metatheorem" —
retracted, see R-2026-05-18._
****

Owning "it is just `fib`" loudly is a credibility asset: it pre-empts
the reviewer's first and strongest objection.

== The five pillars

Each pillar names the artefact it must ship and the *authority effect*
— the specific reason a referee or community would upgrade their
assessment.

=== Pillar A — Pin the identity

`Echo ≃ fib` currently lives in prose (`docs/gate-1-distinct-phenomenon.adoc`).
Reviewers want the bridge as a checked lemma.

* Artefact: `proofs/agda/EchoFiberBridge.agda` — `echo↔fib :
  Echo f y ↔ fiber f y` (definitional; `refl`-level round-trips).
  Pinned in `Smoke.agda`, wired into `All.agda`. *(landed — see
  §"Status".)*
* Authority effect: converts the project's most honest admission into
  a machine-checked artefact. Referees trust projects that formalise
  their own deflationary claims.

=== Pillar B — The universal property

The single biggest lever. Echo has functoriality (`map-over` + id/comp),
the composition iso, cancel-iso, pentagon — but no _characterisation_.

* Artefact 1: `EchoPullback.agda` — `Echo f y` as the pullback of `f`
  along `y : ⊤ → B`, with the terminal-cone universal property
  `echo-pullback-univ`. The categorical-semantics anchor. *Landed
  2026-05-17:* `EchoCone`/`echo-cone`, the `SliceHom`↔cone bridge
  (`cone→slice`/`slice→cone` with `refl` round-trips — the "in
  disguise" claim is now a checked fact), `IsMediator`, and
  `echo-pullback-univ` (existence + funext-free pointwise
  uniqueness, no K — uniqueness uses stdlib `Σ-≡,≡→≡` on the
  `factor`/`coherent` legs).
* Artefact 2: `EchoGradedComonad.agda` — reuse `EchoGraded._≤g_`,
  `_⊔g_`, `≤g-prop`; `gextract` (the identity coercion, `keep` being
  the definitional bottom), `gduplicate` (the join-left *single-grade*
  reindex), and the three coherence equations
  (`gcomonad-counit-{l,r}`, `gcomonad-coassoc`). [RETRACTED READING:
  "the three graded-comonad laws" / "is a graded comonad" — these are
  thin-poset reindexing-coherence equations; no comonad is exhibited.
  See <<h2-verdict>> and R-2026-05-18.]
* Authority effect (narrowed): "has a definitional identity + a
  funext-relative pointwise mediator property + a reindexing modality
  whose coherence reduces to order-thinness" — exactly bounded, not a
  universal property and not a graded comonad.

=== Pillar C — An irreducibility metatheorem

Harden Gate 2. The existing `VisibleConstraintAudit` /
`IntegrationAudit` _negative_ exhibits (collapsing pseudo-theorems to
`proj₂`) are exemplary practice; go one step further.

* Artefact: `EchoSeparating.agda` — a *separating model*: a structure
  satisfying generic Σ-functoriality in which the loss-grade
  composition law (`degrade-compose` / `degrade-via-join`) fails.
  *Landed 2026-05-17:* the model is `EchoGraded` with its single
  hypothesis `≤g-prop` removed — a non-propositional loss order
  (`sep-order-not-prop : ¬ (s₀ ≡ s₁)`) with a reindexing that
  distinguishes the two `lo→hi` arrows. `sep-map-over-id` /
  `sep-map-over-comp` (generic Σ-functoriality) and the functorial
  unit hold verbatim; `sep-degrade-compose-fails : ¬
  SepDegradeCompose` refutes the characteristic law at the checked
  witness `true ≢ false`. Conclusion: `degrade-compose` is carried
  precisely by propositionality of the loss order — genuine
  structure, not Σ-bookkeeping.
* Authority effect: answers "isn't this all Σ-lemmas with new names?"
  with a model, not an argument. Separating models are the gold
  standard for "this structure is real, not bookkeeping".

=== Pillar D — A second model + conservativity

A notion is established only if it is model-independent.

* Artefact 1: `EchoRelModel.agda` — `GradedLossModel`/`GCLaws`,
  instantiated at `set-model` and `rel-model`. [RETRACTED READING:
  "two independent models = robustness / the strongest
  model-independence form". Both instances fix the *same* grade poset;
  `rel-model`'s carrier is the Set carrier `× ⊤`; `model-agreement`
  is `refl`; the interface's `⊑-prop` field bakes in the only
  load-bearing hypothesis. This is *carrier-parametricity over a
  fixed poset*, not model-independence. R-2026-05-18.]
* Artefact 2: `docs/echo-types/conservativity.adoc` — because
  `Echo = Σ` definitionally and the suite is postulate-free under
  `--safe --without-K` (CI-grep-enforced), this is *evidence that*
  the loss modality adds no strength over MLTT+Σ. [RETRACTED READING:
  "State this as a metatheorem" / "a conservativity theorem". A green
  `--safe` build is necessary but not sufficient for proof-theoretic
  conservativity; no meta-argument is mechanised, so this is *not*
  stated as a metatheorem. Funext correction: there is no funext
  anywhere; `examples/Transport.agda` *avoids* it by carrier choice
  rather than "quarantining" it — the real funext-relativity is the
  pointwise-only mediator property. R-2026-05-18.]
* Authority effect (narrowed): an exactly-bounded, postulate-free
  package under a falsifiable retraction discipline — not
  model-independence plus a conservativity theorem.

== Sequencing and guardrails

*Order (revised 2026-05-17 — de-risk first):* A ✓ →
**B(graded comonad, thin slice) ✓ → B(pullback) ✓** →
C(separating model) ✓ → D(second model + conservativity) ✓ →
E(write-up). The graded-comonad result was the pivotal bet, so a
minimal slice was attempted _before_ the safe pullback work to test
the thesis early (fail fast); it settled positively and
over-delivered all three laws, after which the pullback, the
separating model and the second model + conservativity landed as
planned. *Pillars A–D are now complete — the entire internal
programme is done; only Pillar E (external validation) remains.*
A–C were the credibility core and needed no external unblocker. The
ordinal / Buchholz
track is impressive _consumer evidence_, not foundation — keep it
firewalled from the identity claim exactly as `roadmap.adoc` already does.

*Hard guardrails (credibility hazards):*

* Never add a postulate or drop `--safe --without-K` to force a
  "stronger" result — the conservativity _is_ the product.
* The funext boundary is real and known (`examples/Transport.agda`
  stalls because `Field = Fin n → ℚ` forces funext, unavailable under
  `--safe`). Quarantine any funext-requiring result behind an
  explicitly labelled, isolated assumption module; never let it touch
  the core.
* Do *not* reopen the five-axis simultaneous-integration claim — EI-2
  settled it negatively, and having cleanly killed it is part of the
  authority story.

== H2 verdict — does graded coassociativity need path algebra? (2026-05-17)

[CAUTION]
====
*Reading reversed 2026-05-18 (R-2026-05-18).* The collapse described
below is real and the Agda is unchanged — but its significance is the
*opposite* of "thesis-strengthening". The equations reduce to
`degrade-compose` + `≤g-prop` because *there is no comonadic
coherence to discharge*: a thin poset with a functorial monotone
action satisfies them definitionally. This is the calibration that
shows the modality's entire content over generic Σ is thinness of the
grade order — confirmed by the separating model and by the repo's own
Gate-2 audit. Read "graded-comonad laws" below as
"thin-poset reindexing-coherence equations", and disregard the
"*Thesis impact (positive)*" paragraph (struck through in substance,
retained for the record).
====

*The pivotal de-risking question, answered: NO.* The thin slice
(`gextract`, `gduplicate`, `gcomonad-counit-l`, `gcomonad-counit-r`,
`gcomonad-coassoc`) typechecks under `--safe --without-K` with no
postulates. All three graded-comonad laws — including
coassociativity — collapse to `EchoGraded.degrade-compose` plus
`≤g-prop`, the *exact two ingredients* the repo already owns (they
already carry `degrade-via-join`). No new path algebra; no `subst`;
no transport.

The one substantive design point: a *naive* coassociativity
phrasing comparing `GEcho ((g₁⊔g₂)⊔g₃)` with `GEcho (g₁⊔(g₂⊔g₃))`
would force a `subst GEcho (⊔g-assoc …)` transport. The slice
instead states every law in the *common-upper-bound idiom* —
post-compose each (co)multiplication with the universal degrade into
an arbitrary common bound `g'`, so both sides land in the same
`GEcho g'`. The transport then never appears, precisely because the
join is a universal arrow and `_≤g_` is propositional. This is the
same idiom `EchoGraded.degrade-via-join` already uses; it is a
faithful (not weakened) statement of the comonad coherence in the
join-semilattice presentation.

*Thesis impact — RETRACTED 2026-05-18.* This paragraph originally
read: "the 'characterised graded comonad of structured loss' claim
rests cleanly on machinery the repo has already proved… the thesis
stands and is *strengthened*; no honest rewrite is required." That
conclusion was wrong. There is no graded-comonad strength; the
collapse to thinness is a *bound*, not a strengthening. An honest
rewrite *was* required and has been done (`paper.adoc`,
`conservativity.adoc`, `types-abstract.adoc`, R-2026-05-18). The
narrowed thesis (<<thesis>>) is what stands.

NOTE: A 2-categorical refinement of this picture was considered as
a roadmap-level alternative and *ruled out* on the same evidence
(every would-be 2-cell appears as `refl` or is prop-forced trivial
by `≤g-prop` / `⊑-prop`). → ruled out, see
`decisions/no-2-cat.adoc`.
