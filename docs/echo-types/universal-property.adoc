== The arc, in one paragraph

`Echo f y` IS the fibre of `f : A → B` at `y` (definitional;
`EchoFiberBridge.echo↔fib`). Two universal-property questions
follow: is `Echo f y` characterised as a pullback? and is the
total Echo space the canonical (equivalence, projection)
factorisation of `f`? The honest answer to BOTH is: yes under
function extensionality, and yes pointwise (K-free) without it.
The narrative here threads the four artefacts (pullback +
factorisation, each at K-free + funext-qualified levels) into one
story.

the *fibre* of the right-leg projection at `y`, and the right-leg
projection is the canonical `proj₁` out of the total Σ-space.

== Pullback presentation (`EchoPullback.agda`)

`Echo f y` is the pullback of `f` along the point `y : ⊤ → B`:

* A cone over the cospan `(f : A → B, y : ⊤ → B)` from apex `V`
  is the data of `(g : V → A, h : V → ⊤, square : ∀ v → f (g v)
  ≡ y)`. Under `⊤`'s η, `h` is forced (constantly `tt`), so the
  cone collapses to `(g, square)`.
* `EchoPullback.SliceHom (λ _ → y) f` *is* a cone (the two
  round-trips reduce to `refl`).
* `EchoPullback.echo-pullback-univ` exhibits the mediator
  `m : V → Echo f y` with `IsMediator f y c m` (the triangle
  commutes for `m`) and the *pointwise* uniqueness clause
  `∀ (m' : V → Echo f y) → IsMediator f y c m' → ∀ v → m' v ≡ m v`.

The pointwise uniqueness clause is K-free, postulate-free, and
funext-free. It is NOT the terminal-cone universal property
`m' ≡ m` — that requires funext to STATE (function equality
isn't pointwise equality without funext). The honest claim,
mechanised at the K-free level, is the pointwise mediator
property.

== R-2026-05-18 narrowing

Earlier drafts of `paper.adoc` / `types-abstract.adoc` claimed
the unconditional terminal-cone universal property — `m' ≡ m`.
The retraction R-2026-05-18 (`docs/retractions.adoc`) corrects
that to the pointwise mediator property the Agda actually
proves. The discipline:

* The pointwise content `∀ v → m' v ≡ m v` is mechanised,
  honest, and load-bearing — that is what the Agda artefact
  shows.
* The function-equation form `m' ≡ m` is `[RETRACTED]` at the
  unconditional level — it is not a theorem of the development
  without funext.
* The funext-qualified form `funext → m' ≡ m` is earn-backable
  — see Gate F4 below.

This is the canonical "honest-conditional" pattern across the
repo's Pillar F gates: take the missing hypothesis as an
explicit module parameter, never as a postulate, and pin both
the conditional strict form AND the unconditional pointwise
form.

== Gate F4 — pullback strict terminality (`EchoPullbackUnivF4.agda`)

Pillar F's fourth gate (`docs/echo-types/earn-back-plan.adoc`
§"Gate F4 — Universal property, honestly qualified") closes
the pullback's strict terminality conditionally. Module
parameter `FunExt₀` (a level-0 funext hypothesis), one line of
proof:

[source,agda]
----
echo-pullback-univ-strict :
  {A B : Set} (f : A → B) (y : B) {V : Set} (c : EchoCone f y V) →
  Σ (V → Echo f y) (λ m →
    IsMediator f y c m
    × (∀ (m' : V → Echo f y) → IsMediator f y c m' → m' ≡ m))
echo-pullback-univ-strict f y c with echo-pullback-univ f y c
... | m , med , uniq-pt =
      m , med , λ m' med' → funext (uniq-pt m' med')
----

Three lines: take the pointwise mediator property,
apply the supplied `funext` to lift to a function equation.

The unconditional `echo-pullback-univ-pointwise` is kept
verbatim as the funext-free corollary. Reading:

* unconditional, zero hypotheses → pointwise mediator
  property;
* conditional on `funext` → strict terminal cone.

Logged as retraction follow-up F-2026-05-18a.

== Gate F5 — full OFS, honestly qualified (2026-05-27)

The factorisation-system universal property is the larger
sibling of F4. Where F4 closes the pullback's strict
terminality, F5 closes the full (equivalence, projection)
orthogonal factorisation system on Type — factorisation
existence + diagonal lifting + factorisation uniqueness up to
iso. Same F4 template throughout (funext as explicit module
parameter, never a postulate; unconditional pointwise
corollaries kept funext-free).

=== Slice F5-1 (`EchoOFSUnivF5.agda`)

Strict factorisation triangle: `f ≡ proj₁ ∘ encode f` as a
function equation, given funext. Three-line lift of the
existing K-free pointwise `EchoOrthogonalFactorizationSystem.
echo-factorisation`.

=== Slice F5-2 (`EchoOFSUnivF5Diag.agda`)

Diagonal lifting property. Given a commutative square with
left equivalence (via `HasInverse`) + right projection (=
`proj₁`) + pointwise commutativity, the canonical lift
`λ x → h (e⁻¹ x)` exists, satisfies both triangles
pointwise, is unique pointwise. K-free in `module Pointwise`;
strict in `module Strict (funext)`.

[source]
----
                    h
         A ────────────────→ Σ B (Echo f)
         │  ╲                      │
         │    ╲ lift               │
       e │     ╲ (= h ∘ e⁻¹)       │ proj₁
         │       ╲                 │
         ↓         ╲↓               ↓
         A' ────────────────────→ B
                    k

   Premise:   proj₁ ∘ h ≡ k ∘ e  (pointwise commutativity)
   Lift:      ∃! lift such that  lift ∘ e ≡ h   AND   proj₁ ∘ lift ≡ k
   Construct: lift x := h (e⁻¹ x)
   Strictness: function-equation form given funext;
              pointwise form K-free unconditionally.
----

=== Slice F5-3 (`EchoOFSUnivF5Iso.agda`)

Factorisation uniqueness up to iso. Given any second
(equivalence, projection) factorisation `f = p ∘ g` with `g`
an equivalence, construct a canonical `φ : X ↔ Σ B (Echo f)`
with `φ.to ∘ g ≡ encode f` and `proj₁ ∘ φ.to ≡ p`, both
strict given funext.

*Design note.* The natural-but-blocked direct formulation
`φ.to x = (p x, g⁻¹ x, witness)` surfaces a half-adjoint
triangle-identity obligation on the round-trip. The cleaner
construction factors through the existing totality completion:

    φ.to   = encode f ∘ g⁻¹
    φ.from = g ∘ decode f

Round-trips reduce via the K-free `encode-decode` /
`decode-encode` lemmas + `cong` of the inverse data. No
triangle identity needed — bare `HasInverse` suffices.

[source]
----
   Setup: f = p ∘ g, g has two-sided inverse g⁻¹

                     g
        A ────────────────────→ X
        │                       │
   f    │                       │ p
        │                       │
        ↓                       ↓
        B ←─────────────────── B
                  =

   Canonical iso (Gate F5-3):

                  φ.to = encode f ∘ g⁻¹
        X ──────────────────────────────→ Σ B (Echo f)
                                              │
                                              │ proj₁
                  φ.from = g ∘ decode f        │
        X ←──────────────────────────────── Σ B (Echo f)
                                              │
                                              ↓
                                              B
                  (proj₁ ∘ φ.to ≡ p, pointwise via commute)
----

== What stays open

* *(epi, mono) image factorisation.* The "lower" form of the
  factorisation pair, requiring propositional truncation
  `∥_∥`. Not available under `--safe --without-K` without
  HITs or postulated `∥_∥`. Honest-scoped in
  `EchoImageFactorization`'s companion-remark; documented as
  the next earn-back gate.
* *Coherent-equivalence upgrade of `HasInverse`.* A half-
  adjoint triangle identity field would let some of the F5-3
  path-algebra become definitional rather than pointwise.
  Deferred refinement; not load-bearing for F5 as it stands
  (the composition design works around the absence).
* *Real categorical OFS in a 2-categorical setting.* Echo's
  OFS witness is at the (Type, function) level; a
  2-categorical refinement would carry the iso between two
  factorisations as part of the data. Out of scope under
  `--safe --without-K` without 2-category machinery.
